# Introduction: This Book Isn't for Everyone
### "The truth won't make you comfortable. But comfort has already cost you more than you know."

---

You're holding a book that is going to irritate you.

Not because it's poorly written. Not because the ideas are wrong. You're going to be irritated because it will say things you already know but have been pretending you don't. And you're going to be even more irritated because it won't give you a plan.

No five steps. No morning routine checklist. No seven habits of highly effective whatever. Just an honest conversation about how you think — and why that system you've been running isn't working for you.

This book is not for you if you want to feel good. This book is for you if you want to feel real.

If you picked this up hoping someone would tell you that you're doing fine just the way you are, put it down. Someone else will write that book. It will sell very well. And six months later you'll be sitting in the same chair, with the same vague dissatisfaction, having learned nothing about why it's there.

This book is also not for you if you want a quick fix. If you're looking for the three secrets that will transform your life by Tuesday. Close the cover. The people who sell three secrets rarely have one.

But if you're 22 and wondering why you feel behind when you can't even define "ahead," this book is for you. If you're 26 and you've built a life that looks correct from the outside but feels borrowed from the inside, this book is for you. If you've caught yourself comparing your entire existence to someone's carefully curated highlight reel and wondering what's wrong with you — there is nothing wrong with you. You're just running software that was installed before you had the chance to choose it.

That's what this book is about. Not what to do. Not how to act. How you think. The mental models you've inherited without inspection. The beliefs you carry like they were always yours when someone handed them to you before you knew what a belief was.

I've watched smart people destroy good years because they never stopped to ask whether the thinking driving them was actually theirs. I've watched talented people shrink themselves down to fit into boxes built by people who didn't know them. I've watched people get to 30, 35, 40 and suddenly realize they have no idea who made the decisions that got them there.

This book won't let you do that without at least knowing you're doing it.

I'm not going to tell you my credentials. I'm not going to tell you about the retreats I've led or the clients I've coached. I don't care about that and neither should you. What matters is whether what I'm saying here is true — and whether it's useful. You can decide that in the next few pages.

If you decide I'm wrong, fine. Disagree out loud. Argue with the page. That alone puts you ahead of most people who will read this and nod along while changing nothing.

If you decide I'm right — not about everything, not about me, but about yourself — then something in here has to move. You can't read a book called "Nobody Told Me This" and stay exactly the same.

That's the deal. I'm going to be honest with you. More honest than feels comfortable. And you're going to have to decide what to do with that.

Turn the page if you're ready.
