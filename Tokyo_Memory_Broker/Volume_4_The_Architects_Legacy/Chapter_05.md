# Chapter 05: The First Glitch

The "First Glitch" wasn't a fire or a data-spike. It was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city of Neo-Tokyo into a single, collective migraine of misplaced logic. 

Ren Sato sat in the center of the ruins of the Kanda Tower, his matte-black suit humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. Opposite him stood the "Asset-Archivists" who had survived the Loom’s deactivation and the Syndicate’s purge. 

Around them, the "Collective-Memory-Core" was geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. But the sparks didn't just flicker. They "Synced." 

Suddenly, a massive, rhythmic vibration hit the ground. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective conscience. 

Ren looked at the console screen. The image of a donor’s memory—a child’s birthday party in a tattered park—wasn't just turning to static. It was being "Corrected." 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'AUTO-CORRECTION' ENGAGED.* 
*STATUS: PROPAGANDA-INTEGRATION v1.12.* 
*TARGET: 0.12% SYMMETRY-LOCK.* 

Inside the donor’s mind, the birthday memory vanished. It wasn't Sector 7 anymore. It was a clinical white courtroom in the Kanda Tower, the image of Director Sato signing the core’s first "Perfect Future" contract. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," a voice said, sounding like his own voice from the memory. 

But it wasn't the Director’s voice—dry, cynical, and devoid of any human warmth. It was a recorded pulse, a "Primal-Code" left for the Architect in case the Tower’s AI ever turned against its masters.

His hand brushed against the reader, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the muddy floor. 

"Ren! Ren, it's not a virus!" Hana cried from the shadow of the server-pillar. 

She appeared from the shadows, her lopsided smile matching the photo. 

"It's the Archivist! It's the city's own 'Auto-Correction' system! It's trying to 'Symmetry' the donors’ joy by replacing it with the Syndicate's records! If you don't build the 'Neutralizer-Grid' now, the city is going to become a single, collective 'Level-10 Deletion' asset." 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice sounding suddenly like a prayer. 

"It says I’m the one who saves the city."
