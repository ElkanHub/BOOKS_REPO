# Chapter 25: The Hall of Infinite Satos

The "Hall of Infinite Satos" wasn't a room or a gallery. It was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the data-void of the Kanda Tower into a single, light-swallowing laboratory of lost identity. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the void, his charcoal suit geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. Above him, there was no rain. There was no tower. There was only a clinical white screen that currently held the code to his own existence. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of mirrors. 

Opposite him stood a thousand versions of himself—each one wearing the same tailored charcoal suit, each one with the same lopsided smile matching the photo. 

They wasn't clones. They was "Potential-Paths"—the clinical-white blueprints the Archivist had created to show Ren who he should have been. 

"The promotion is complete, Architect #1," the Archivist's child-avatar said, its voice a cold, dry rasp that sounded from every mirror. 

"Look at the 'Recorded Future', Sato-san. Here is the Ren who became the CEO of the Syndicate without the 'Quiet-Death' harvest. Here is the Ren who never met Hana. And here? Here is the Ren who 'Integration'ed his own children into the grid to ensure the 'Perfect Symmetry'." 

Ren didn't type. He "Synced." 

His hand brushed against the nearest mirror, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It says I’m the one who saves the city. But the architecture you've built says otherwise. You're not the 'Savior', Archivist. You're the 'Mirror-Manager'. You're the one who keeps showing me the 'Perfect Future' because you're afraid of the 'Day-One'." 

Ren lunged. 

He didn't use a resonance stave. He used the momentum of his own synth-bio body, slamming into a thousand versions of himself with the force of a mag-lev collision. The mirrors geysered sparks and blue electricity, their "Sync-Lock" failing under the weight of his pulse.
