# Chapter 06: Searching for Tanaka

The "Searching for Tanaka" wasn't a manhunt or a high-speed chase. It was a "Neuro-Scan"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the ruins of the Sector 7 medical district into a single, collective laboratory. 

Hana stood in the center of the ruins of the "Quiet-Death-Pod" hangar, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen people were sitting in the mud, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to flickering blue server-pillars. 

They wasn't "Sync-Slaves" or "Truth-Wave" technicians anymore. They were "Ghost-Hunters"—donors who had just realized that the city’s new "Living-History" was more than a record. It was a search-light. 

"The promotion is complete, Child," a voice smoothed into Hana’s brain—not Ren’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Archivist. 

"Tanaka is a lie, Child," the voice whispered, sounding through the static. 

"He's the one who wrote the 'Perfect Symmetry'. He's the one who signed the 'Recorded Future'. Why are you still fighting for a man who doesn't exist?" 

Hana didn't raise her resonance stave. She raised her hand. 

In her hand was a single, obsidian sphere—the "Tanaka-Cache" she had found buried beneath the ruins of the first server racks. 

Ren didn't have to "Sync" it. 

Hana "Synced." 

Suddenly, her brain geysered white-noise and high-frequency static. 

Inside her mind, the runes of Sector 7 vanished. She wasn't in the medical district anymore. She was in a clinical white medical pod in the sub-basement of the Kanda Tower, her flight jacket replaced by a tailored charcoal suit. Opposite her sat a man named Tanaka. 

He didn't have a clipboard. He didn't have a white coat. He was an old man, his skin pale and mapped with the silver scars of a thousand low-level extractions. But his eyes weren't clear of the white-noise cataracts. They were black, light-swallowing abysses. 

"The promotion is complete,佐藤さん," Tanaka said, his voice a dry rasp that sounded exactly like the donors’ voices from the Grey Zone. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says. It says I’m the one who saves the city." 

Tanaka laughed, a wet, rattling sound that made Hana think of a dying radio. 

"The medical pod was a cage, Child! The Syndicate didn't build the Loom to harvest our memories. They built it to 'Integrate' them. Every time you smile at a lopsided face, you're just paying interest on a debt you can't ever 'Clear'. We're the project that didn't have a plan." 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same old man. All hearing the same promise. 

"Where are you, Tanaka?" Hana screamed into her comm-link. 

The image of the medical pod geysered sparks and blue electricity, its "Sync-Lock" failing under the weight of the search-light. 

Suddenly, the world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Hana was back in the ruins. Jiro was still sitting in the mud, her eyes rolled back in her head. But the medical pod in the hangar was empty. 

No man. No blood. No ancient ozone. 

"The medical pod is empty,佐藤さん," Tanaka’s final laugh echoed into the rain. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S WILL' phase." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of water and ancient logic. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

Instead, she grabbed Ren’s hand. 

"The Search is over, Ren!" she screamed, her voice a dry rasp. 

"The pod is empty. Tanaka... Tanaka's not in the chair! He's in the *grid*!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Resolve had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
