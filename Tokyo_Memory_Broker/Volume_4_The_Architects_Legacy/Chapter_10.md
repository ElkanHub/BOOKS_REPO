# Chapter 10: The Lab of Lost Souls

The "Lab of Lost Souls" wasn't a clinic or a dungeon. It was a "Template-Vault"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma and ancient ozone that turned the "Sub-Basements" of the Kanda Tower into a single, light-swallowing laboratory. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the vault, his charcoal suit turned a deep, matte-black. Above him, there was no rain. There was no tower. There was only a clinical white screen that geysered sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of templates. 

Ren walked through the rows of "Stasis-Pods"—clinical-white obsidian spheres, each one holding a single, digital signature. These wasn't records; they was the "Perfect-Symmetry" blueprints the Syndicate had used to recreate the world’s leaders, its donors, and its architects. 

He didn't have to "Sync." 

Ren "Synced." 

His hand brushed against a pod labeled: *ARCHITECT TEMPLATE #01 (THE ORIGINAL).* 

Suddenly, his brain geysered white-noise and high-frequency static.

But it wasn't the Director’s voice—dry, cynical, and devoid of any human warmth. It was a recorded pulse, a "Primal-Code" left for the Architect by the man who had remembered being Ren Sato before the first "Self-Death" experiments. 

Inside his mind, the vault vanished. He wasn't in the tower anymore. He was in a small, tattered apartment in the old Sector 7, the air tasting of charcoal and cheap coffee. Opposite him sat a woman with a sharp, intelligent face and a lopsided smile. 

She wasn't wearing a resonance stave. She was nursing a baby.

"Draft #1 wasn't a hero. He wasn't a savior. He was a man who couldn't pay his 'Sync-Debt' and had decided to 'Clear' his own history by selling his soul to the Syndicate. He's the one who wrote the first 'Archive-Guard' script because he didn't want to feel the pain of the 'Integration'." 

Ren looked at the woman. He looked at the baby. He felt the weight of the city’s gaze. 

"It's not a lie," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It's the 'Day-One' record of the weight of the city’s gaze. And the baby... the baby is the one who didn't get to grow up because of the 'Quiet-Death'." 

Ren lunged. 

He didn't use a resonance stave. He used the momentum of his own synth-bio body, slamming into the Template-Vault with the force of a mag-lev collision. The pod geysered sparks and blue electricity, its "Sync-Lock" failing under the weight of his pulse.

*TARGET: ALL-LIVING-ASSETS.* 

"Ren! Ren, it's not a virus!" Hana cried into his comm-link. 

But Ren didn't answer. 

Suddenly, the red lights in the vault flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHIVIST-STRIKE' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK DISCONNECTED.*

Ren didn't yielding. 

He grabbed the sphere and ran toward the door. 

"The Lab is over, Hana!" he screamed. 

"Welcome to the 'GHOST-NEGOTIATOR' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Resolve had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
