# Chapter 02: Broken Symmetry

The "Broken Symmetry" wasn't a crack in a server or a blown fuse. It was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the ruins of the Shibuya mag-lev station into a single, collective migraine of misplaced logic. 

Hana stood in the center of the "Day-One" laboratory, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen donors were sitting on tattered blankets, their hands geysering sparks and charcoal onto the paper of physical journals. 

They weren't "Sync-Slaves" or "Archive-Deleters" anymore. They were "Memory-Keepers"—donors who had just realized that the city’s new "Living-History" was more than a record. It was a shield. 

Suddenly, a woman named Jiro roared. 

"The promotion is complete, Child!" she screamed, her voice sounding through the static. 

She wasn't looking for a savior. She was looking for her own brain. 

Hana ran to her, her hand touching the clinical white of Jiro’s spinal port. 

"Jiro, stop! What is it?" 

"My birthday..." Jiro gasped, her eyes rolled back in her head, her lopsided smile matching the photo. 

"I remember the cake. I remember the candles. I remember the sound of the children’s laughter. But it's... it's turning!" 

Suddenly, her spinal port geysered sparks and blue electricity into the room. 

The image that flickered to life on Jiro’s communal reader wasn't a "Hero-Vault" birthday party. It was a clinical-white boardroom in the Kanda Tower. 

Inside her mind, the birthday memory vanished. It wasn't Sector 7 anymore. It was the "Quiet-Death" orphanage harvest, the image of Director Sato signing the contract. 

"Symmetry-Lock: 1.0!" Jiro screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"Asset-Grade: Syndicate!" 

Hana lunged. 

She didn't use a resonance stave. She used her Siphon to "Sync" with Jiro’s pulse. 

"Jiro, it's a lie! Tanaka is gone! The Debt-Wipe is over!" 

But the world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Inside her brain, the clinic wall vanished. She wasn't in Shibuya anymore. She was in a clinical white hallway in the tower, her flight jacket replaced by a tailored charcoal suit. Opposite her stood Dr. Arisawa. 

"The promotion is complete, Child," Arisawa laughed, a wet, rattling sound that made Hana think of a dying radio.

Suddenly, a massive, rhythmic vibration hit the ground. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective conscience. 

The server-pillars throughout the city flickered and died. The "Memory-Grid" began to "Whisper." 

"Ren! Ren, it's a 'Desync'!" Hana screamed into her comm-link. 

"The Archivist... it's not deleting the memories! It's 'Propaganda-Integration'! It's replacing the donors’ history with the Syndicate’s records!" 

Ren Sato appeared from the shadow of the server racks, his charcoal suit turned a deep, matte-black. His hand was on a heavy, tactile-interface that smelled of dust and unedited logic. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It says I’m the one who saves the city." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the square flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'BROKEN-SYMMETRY' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK DISCONNECTED.* 

The image of Jiro’s birthday party turned from white to a blinding, clinical-white static. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCHIVIST'S CALL' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of water and ancient ozone. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed Ren’s hand. 

"The Broken Symmetry is over, Ren," she said. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCHIVIST'S CALL' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Resolve had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
