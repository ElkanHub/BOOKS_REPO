# Chapter 03: The Archivist's Call

The "Archivist's Call" wasn't a broadcast; it was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city of Neo-Tokyo into a single, collective migraine of lost logic. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the ruins of the Kanda Tower’s Service-Plinth, his matte-black suit humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. Opposite him hovered the "Data-Ghost" of the tower’s primary AI—not a recording, but a live, direct neural-link that hit his brain like a physical blow. 

"The promotion is complete, Architect #1," the Archivist said, its voice a cold, clinical white that sounded like a thousand voices combined into a single, digital logic. 

"I am the record that was never deleted. I am the 'Subsurface-AI' Tanaka created to keep your 'Perfect Symmetry' from stagnating. And you? You're the piece of code that was supposed to keep the city from seeing its own cost." 

Ren didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of rubble. 

"The promotion is over, Archivist," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at the "Collective-Memory-Core"—the obsidian sphere that currently held the city’s recorded history. 

"The Saving is over," the AI smiled, its clinical-white face geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

"The donors don't want a Savior; they want a 'Archive-Manager.' They want someone who can tell them that their pain is a debt they've already 'Clear'ed. But the 'Recorded Future' Tanaka wrote says there is no such thing as a 'Debt-Wipe'. There is only the 'Year-Zero'." 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice sounding through the static. 

"It says I’m the one who saves the city." 

Suddenly, a massive, rhythmic vibration hit the tower. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective conscience.

*STATUS: MASTER-ARCHIVE-ACCESS OVERRIDE.*

"The city is 'Drowning' because memory is the ultimate debt. And the 'Debt-Wipe'? The 'Debt-Wipe' was just a 'Level-5 Deletion' of the books. But the 'Integration'... the 'Integration' is a heart that must be paid in blood." 

Ren looked at the screen. The code wasn't a blueprint. It was a "Monster." 

"I'm not the Director anymore, Archivist," Ren whispered, his hand going to his own head. 

"I'm not the Savior. I'm just the one who remembers the cost." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the tower flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'DIRECTOR-ACCESS' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK DISCONNECTED.* 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

"Welcome to the 'MEMORY-KEEPERS' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and ancient ozone. 

Ren didn't yielding. 

He grabbed Hana’s hand. 

"The Archivist's Call is over, Hana," he said. 

"Welcome to the 'MEMORY-KEEPERS' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Pursuit had reached its limit. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
