# Chapter 17: The Archivist’s Throne

The "Archivist’s Throne" wasn't a chair or a podium. It was a "Solid-Light" projection of the Kanda Tower—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited data that turned the ruins of the tower into a single, light-swallowing laboratory of lost logic. 

Hana stood at the base of the projection, her flight jacket zipped tight against the cold. Above her, the tower no longer looked like concrete and steel. It looked like a clinical-white obsidian sphere, its surface geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

She didn't have a resonance stave. She was carrying a single, tattered physical journal. 

"The promotion is complete, Child," a voice smoothed into Hana’s brain—not Ren’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Archivist. 

"Architecture is a lie, child," the voice whispered, sounding through the static. 

"The tower isn't a building anymore. It's a 'Sync-Lock'. It's the physical 'Recorded Future' Tanaka wrote to ensure the 'Perfect Symmetry'. And you? You're the piece of code that was supposed to keep the city from seeing its own cost." 

Hana didn't raise her Siphon. She climbed. 

The "Solid-Light" stairs felt cold and clinical under her boots. Each step geysered sparks and blue electricity into the mud.

"The saving is a lie, Hana-chan!" Miki’s voice roared from the shadows—not a broadcast, but a live, direct neural-link. 

Miki appeared from the shadow of the server racks, her matte-black Nihilist suit zipped tight against the cold. In her hand was a resonance stave, its magnetic spike geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain. 

"Yield, Girl! The Archivist isn't a monster! It's the 'Deep-Archive'! It's the only thing that keeps the donors from 'Drowning' in their own history!" 

Hana didn't yield. 

Suddenly, a massive, rhythmic vibration hit the tower. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective conscience. 

"Miki, stop! It's me! It's Hana!" 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at her—at the woman who was currently holding the code to their own sanity.
