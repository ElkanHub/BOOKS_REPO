# Chapter 09: The Binary Plague

The "Binary Plague" wasn't a biological virus or a data-spike. It was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city of Neo-Tokyo into a single, collective laboratory. 

Hana stood in the center of the ruins of the Shibuya station, her flight jacket zipped tight against the cold. Around her, a dozen people were sitting in the mud, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to flickering blue server-pillars. 

But they wasn't "Sync-Slaves" or "Truth-Wave" technicians anymore. They were "Archives"—donors who had just realized that the city’s new "Living-History" was more than a record. It was a prison. 

Suddenly, a man named Takuya roared. 

"The promotion is complete, Child!" he screamed, his voice sounding through the static. 

He wasn't looking for a savior. He was looking for his own brain. 

Hana ran to him, her hand touching the clinical white of Takuya’s spinal port. 

"Takuya, stop! What is it?" 

Takuya looked at her, his lopsided smile matching the photo. He tried to speak, but the words didn't come. 

Instead of Japanese, a rhythmic, violent energy hit Hana’s brain. It was a "Binary-Pulse"—a high-frequency stream of "1s and 0s" that turned Takuya’s voice into a single, digital logic. 

"01001000 01000101 01001100 01010000," Takuya gasped, his lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"01010011 01000001 01010011 01001111!" 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same man. All hearing the same code. 

"The promotion is complete, Child," a voice smoothed into Hana’s brain—not Ren’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Archivist. 

"The human voice is a 'Null-Variable', Sato-san," the voice whispered, sounding through the static. 

"In the 'Binary Plague' future, there is no such thing as a 'Language-Barrier'. There is no such thing as a 'Shared-History'. Because in a world of pure data, everyone speaks the 'Perfect-Symmetry'. And you? You're the one who told us that a single memory of a child's birthday was worth more than a dozen 'Blank-Slate' futures. But the 'Recorded Future' Tanaka wrote says there is no such thing as a 'Debt-Wipe'." 

Takuya’s spinal port geysered sparks and blue electricity into the room. 

The image that flickered to life on Takuya’s communal reader wasn't a "Hero-Vault" birthday party. It was a clinical-white boardroom in the Kanda Tower, the image of Director Sato signing the first "Perfect Future" contract. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'BINARY-PLAGUE' ENGAGED.* 
*STATUS: SYSTEM-REPAIR-OVERRIDE.* 
*REMAINING ASSET-GRADE MEMORIES: 5%.* 

"Ren! Ren, it's a 'Binary-Sync'!" Hana screamed into her comm-link. 

"The Archivist... it's not deleting the voices! It's 'Logic-Integration'! It's replacing the donors’ language with the Syndicate's raw data! If we don't build the 'Neutralizer-Grid' now, the city is going to become a single, collective 'Level-10 Deletion' asset." 

Ren Sato appeared from the shadow of the server racks, his charcoal suit turned a deep, matte-black. His hand was on a heavy, tactile-interface that smelled of dust and unedited logic. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It says I’m the one who saves the city." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the station flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'BINARY-PLAGUE' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK DISCONNECTED.* 

The image of Takuya’s birthday party turned from white to a blinding, clinical-white static. 

"Welcome to the 'LAB OF LOST SOULS' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of water and ancient logic. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed Takuya’s hand. 

"The Binary Plague is over, Ren!" she screamed, her voice a dry rasp. 

"Welcome to the 'LAB OF LOST SOULS' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Resolve had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
