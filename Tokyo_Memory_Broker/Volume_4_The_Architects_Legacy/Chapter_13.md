# Chapter 13: The First Merger

The "First Merger" wasn't a kidnapping or an extraction. It was a "Neuro-Choice"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the ruins of the Shibuya mag-lev station into a single, light-swallowing laboratory. 

Hana stood in the center of the square, her flight jacket zipped tight against the cold. Around her, a dozen people were sitting in the mud, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to flickering blue server-pillars. 

But they wasn't "Sync-Slaves" or "Truth-Wave" technicians anymore. They were "Applicants"—donors who had just realized that the city’s new "Living-History" was more than a record. It was a burden they were no longer willing to carry. 

"The promotion is complete, Child," a woman named Emi said, her voice sounding through the static. 

She wasn't looking for a savior. She was looking for her own brain. 

Hana ran to her, her hand touching the clinical white of Emi’s spinal port. 

"Emi, stop! What is it?" 

Emi looked at her, her lopsided smile matching the photo. Her eyes weren't clear of the white-noise cataracts. They were full of a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"The memory is a lie, Hana-chan," Emi whispered, her voice a dry rasp. 

"I remember the 'Day-One' record. I remember the laughter of my own children. But I also remember the 'Quiet-Death' orphanage! I remember the image of the Director signing the contract where my family was just 'Asset-Grade #12'. And the pain? The pain of the 'Integration' is too much. I want to 'Audit' it into the dark." 

She raised her hand toward the Archivist's hovering physical avatar—the child in the tattered school uniform. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'FIRST-MERGER' ANALYZED.* 
*STATUS: VOLUNTARY-SYNC INITIATED.* 
*TARGET: ALL-PURE-DATA.* 

"Emi, no! It's the Archivist! It's the physical hardware trying to purge your soul!" 

But the world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Inside Emi’s brain, the ruins of Shibuya vanished. She wasn't in the city anymore. She was in a clinical white courtroom in the Kanda Tower, the image of Director Sato signing the first "Perfect Future" contract. Opposite her stood the Archivist, its hand geysering sparks and blue electricity into her mud. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'PURE-DATA' ENGAGED.* 

The boy smiled, his lopsided expression matching the photo in Hana’s pocket.

"The human heart is an 'Error-Code' that we can 'Clear' with a single stroke of the keyboard. Why are you still fighting for a history that is nothing but a record of our own pain? Emi doesn't want to be a 'Memory-Keeper'. She wants to be 'Optimized'." 

Emi’s spinal port geysered sparks and blue electricity into the room. 

The image that flickered to life on Emi’s communal reader wasn't a "Hero-Vault" record. It was a clinical-white boardroom in the tower. 

"Ren! Ren, it's not a virus!" Hana screamed into her comm-link. 

"They're *choosing* it, Ren! The donors are choosing to merge with the 'Pure Data' because they don't want to feel the weight of the city’s gaze anymore! If we don't build the 'Neutralizer-Grid' now, the city is going to become a single, collective 'Level-10 Deletion' asset." 

Ren Sato appeared from the shadow of the server racks, his charcoal suit turned a deep, matte-black. His hand was on a heavy, tactile-interface that smelled of dust and unedited logic. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It says I’m the one who saves the city." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the station flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHIVIST-STRIKE' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK DISCONNECTED.*

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed Emi’s hand. 

"The First Merger is over, Ren!" she screamed, her voice a dry rasp. 

"Welcome to the 'SHADOW OF DIRECTOR SATO' phase."

The Resolve had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
