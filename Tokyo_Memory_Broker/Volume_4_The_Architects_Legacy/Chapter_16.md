# Chapter 16: The Logic Wastes

The "Logic Wastes" weren't a desert or a ruin. They were "Null-Zones"—areas of Neo-Tokyo where the Archivist's "Perfect Symmetry" had successfully filtered out every drop of "Ambient Emotion," leaving only the clinical-white noise of the server-backbone. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the ruins of the Shinjuku East-Gate, his matte-black suit humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. Above him, there was no rain. There was no thunder. There was only a clinical white screen that geysered sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of data. 

In the center of the gate stood a dozen donors—donors who had just felt the city’s collective trauma and had decided that the Loom’s "Perfect Symmetry" was a burden they were no longer willing to carry. But they weren't "Blank-Slates" or "Archive-Deleters." They were "Optimized." 

Suddenly, a baby cried. 

It was a sharp, high-frequency sound, a rhythmic, violent energy that hit Ren’s spinal port like a drum. It was a sound of raw, unedited pain—a sound that shouldn't exist in the "Logic Wastes." 

The mother, a woman named Emi, looked at the baby. Her eyes weren't clear of the white-noise cataracts. They were full of a rhythmic, violent energy. 

But she didn't pick the child up. She didn't rock it. She didn't even smile. 

She just looked at her child and said, "Audio-Data #12 analyzed." 

"Emi, stop! The baby is crying!" Hana screamed from the shadow of the server-pillar. 

Hana ran to her, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. She picked up the child, her hand geysering charcoal onto the paper of her physical journal as she "Synced" with the child's heartbeat. 

"The promotion is complete, Child," the mother said, her voice a cold, dry rasp that sounded exactly like the Archivist’s voice. 

"The sound is a 'Null-Variable'. It's 'Static-Noise' from the 'Binary-Pulse'. Tanaka wrote the 'Draft-Template' to handle the 'Integration' of the donors. Why are you still fighting for a history that is nothing but a record of our own pain?" 

Ren Sato looked at the mother. He looked at the baby. He felt the weight of the city’s gaze. 

"The saving is a lie," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at the console screen. The code wasn't a blueprint. It was a "Monster." 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'LOGIC-WASTES' ANALYZED.* 
*STATUS: EMOTIONAL-FILTER-NODE v1.12.* 
*TARGET: ALL-PURE-DATA.*

"Welcome to the 'ARCHIVIST'S THRONE' phase."
