# Chapter 13: The Mirror’s Edge

The "Mirror’s Edge" was a ledge of high-frequency white-noise that overlooked the Core-Archives. It was the only part of the Kanda Tower where the Master-Loom’s signal geysered into the dark, a rhythmic, violent energy that made the air itself taste like ozone and old, unedited data. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the ledge, his charcoal suit whipping in the wind. Opposite him stood Archivist Kyo. 

The man in the tattered lab-coat didn't raise his resonance stave. He raised his eyes—eyes that were clear of the white-noise cataracts, but were devoid of any human warmth. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," Kyo smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"Did you find the 'Quiet-Death' memory? Did you see the Accountant’s work?" 

Ren didn't answer. He was staring at the Shard in his hand—the fragmented, "Black-Project" Shard. 

"The village was an error, Sato-san," Kyo laughed, a wet, rattling sound that made Ren think of a dying radio. 

"They were all 'Asset-Grade' donors, but their Loom-Symmetry was too... loud. They weren't just remembering their own lives; they were 'Syncing' with the future before we’d even written it. We had to delete them to keep the Architect from becoming 'Static-Headed'." 

Ren felt the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes the city," he smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"Is it a lie, Kyo?" Ren asked, his hand going to the siler-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"Or is the 'Hero-Vault' the lie? The savior of children, the pillar of the community... was any of that real?" 

Kyo stepped closer, his finger touching the clinical white of his own visor. 

"The 'Hero' memories were a gift, Sato-san," Kyo smoothed, his voice sounding suddenly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

"Tanaka gave them to you to keep you sane. He knew the 'Architect-Template' was too valuable to kill, but he also knew the Director couldn't live with the cost of the harvest. So... he gave you a 'Hero-Loop' to play during the resets. He gave you a past that you could believe in." 

Ren looked at the Shard. He looked at Kyo—at the man who had been his "Shadow." 

"You didn't reset me as punishment, then," Ren whispered, his eyes fixing on the screen. 

"No," Kyo laughed. "We reset you because you were 'Drowning.' You were starting to care about the donors. You were starting to care about Arisawa's girl. We had to delete the empathy to keep the Loom’s 'Perfect Symmetry' from shattering." 

Suddenly, a sharp, cold sensation hit Ren’s ear. His comm-link buzzed into life—not the Syndicate channel, but a private frequency he didn't recognize. 

"Ren! Ren, come on!" 

It was Hana. Her voice was frantic, sounding through the static. 

"I found it, Ren! The 'Hidden-Feed'! Tanaka is... he's in the 'Processing Ward'! He’s using Taku to anchor the new 'Architect-Template'!" 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"The Mirror’s Edge is over, Kyo," Ren said, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says." 

Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Sato-san," the Director whispered. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Ren grabbed his Spike-Knife and dove through the drainage tunnel that led to the mag-lev tracks. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Mirror’s Edge was over. The Pursuit had begun.
