# Chapter 09: The Ghost in the Machine

The "Draft-Archive" was a cathedral of flickering neon and the smell of ancient ozone. It sat on the periphery of the Core-Archives, a room filled with a dozen massive, clinical-white server racks that stored the Syndicate’s unedited, low-grade data. 

Hana stood in the center of the archive, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, malevolent blue. Beside her, Ren was exploring the "Hero-Vault," his mind already integrated into the Syndicate’s polished narratives. 

"The tower isn't just a link, Ren," Hana whispered, her eyes fixed on the terminal screen. 

The screen was a maze of high-frequency white-noise and molten hardware. But beneath the noise, she could see the "Loom’s-Fingerprints"—the raw, unedited drafts of the world’s history. 

She began to hack the peripheral files, her fingers moving with a fluid, mechanical grace that felt more like the Broker’s than her own. She was looking for Taku, but what she found was something else. 

She found the "Revision-Cloud"—the place where the Syndicate kept the stories that didn't make the cut. 

"September 12, 2084: Draft #1," a file label read. 

Hana opened it. 

The image that flickered to life on the grainy CRT monitor wasn't the "Hero-Vault" image of Director Sato. It was a darker, more clinical version. In this version, the Director wasn't handing out Shards; he was taking them. He was standing in a high-end Shinjuku office, his hand on the shoulder of a young man with a sharp, intelligent face. 

The man in the photo wasn't an executive. He was an Enforcer. 

"Protéget #112," Hana whispered, her eyes fixing on the file. 

"Record of First Harvest: September 12, 2084." 

She felt the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"The Amnesty Order isn't a gift, Ren," she hissed, her eyes fixing on the Master-Loom. 

"It's a 'Sync-Lock.' Tanaka is using the original dates to anchor the donors to their own trauma, turning them into a single, collective memory of the Syndicate’s victory." 

She moved to the next file. It was a directory labeled *TEMPLATES: ARCHITECT CLASS.* 

Hana opened it. 

The screen flickered once, then turned a clinical white. 

Inside the directory were a dozen files, each one containing a high-resolution 3D scan of a human face. 

Every face was identical. 

They all had the sharp, intelligent face and the lopsided smile of Ren Sato. 

But each one was marked with a different "Draft-Number." 

*ARCHITECT #1: DIRECTOR GRADE (Active)* 
*ARCHITECT #2: BROKER GRADE (Deleted)* 
*ARCHITECT #3: FUGITIVE GRADE (Active)* 

"Ren..." Hana whispered, her hand going to her mouth. 

"The man in the photo... he's not a person. He's a 'Class-A' asset. A template the Syndicate uses to rebuild the city every time the Loom crashes." 

Suddenly, a sharp, cold sensation hit Hana’s ear. Her comm-link buzzed into life—not the Syndicate channel, but a private frequency she didn't recognize. 

"Yield, Girl," a voice said, sounding like a thousands of voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"I won't yield," Hana said, her voice a dry rasp. 

She didn't raise her Siphon. She raised her hand. 

In her hand was a small, tattered print—a physical photo, identical to the one she’d shown Ren. 

"Recorded on September 12, 2084," Hana whispered. 

"Yes," the Loom said, its thoughts a rhythmic, violent deluge. "Because today is the day the future begins. Today is the day the Loom’s 'Perfect Symmetry' becomes the only timeline."

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Hana-chan," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

Instead, she grabbed her Siphon and slammed it into the server racks. 

"Ren!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"They're clones! You're all clones!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Ghost in the Machine was over. The Pursuit had begun.
