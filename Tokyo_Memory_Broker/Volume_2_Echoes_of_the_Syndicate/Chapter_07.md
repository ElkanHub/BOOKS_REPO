# Chapter 07: The Gatekeeper

The entrance to the "Core-Archives" was a portal of polished obsidian and a smell of jasmine and sterilized gold. It sat at the very heart of the Kanda Tower, a room encased in a single, massive block of lead-glass that shielded it from the Syndicate’s primary sensor-grid. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the room, his charcoal suit whipping in the wind. Beside him, Hana was breathing in shallow, rhythmic bursts, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue as she scanned the internal resonance of the tower. 

In the center of the portal, suspended in a magnetic cradle, was a single obsidian sphere. It was the size of a human head, and it pulsed with a soft, bioluminescent blue. 

The Mind of the Archive. 

Ren moved toward the sphere, his hands steady. He could feel the Loom’s reach—not as a tech-grid, but as a "Linguistics-Field." It was trying to speak to him, its thoughts a rhythmic, violent deluge that bypassed his neurons and hit his soul. 

"Yield, Sato-san," a voice said, sounding like a thousands of voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"I won't yield," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

Suddenly, a figure stepped out from the shadow of the server-pillar. 

It wasn't an Enforcer. It wasn't Tanaka. It was a man in a tattered lab-coat, his skin mapped with the silver scars of a thousand low-level extractions. 

Kyo. 

He was the Syndicate’s lead Archivist, the man who had been the Director's "Shadow" during the original riot in '81. He was the one who had harvested the first "Self-Death" experiments. 

Ren stopped. His hand went to his weapon. 

"Kyo," Ren said, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," Kyo smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"Welcome home, Director. We've been expecting you." 

He didn't raise his resonance stave. He raised his hand. 

In his hand was a small, tattered print—a physical photo, identical to the one Hana had. 

"September 12, 2084," Kyo whispered, his eyes fixing on the photo. 

"Yes," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"Because today is the day the future begins. Today is the day the Loom’s 'Perfect Symmetry' becomes the only timeline." 

Ren felt the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"The Amnesty Order is for 'Asset-Grade' donors, Sato-san," Kyo laughed, a wet, rattling sound. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Sat-san." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the room flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHITECT-RECOVERY' ENGAGED. ASSET #1 VERIFIED.* 

Ren Sato felt his heart—or the mechanical pump that served as his heart—racing. 

He looked at the portal. He looked at Kyo—at the man who had been his "Shadow." 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his hand going to the siler-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes you," Ren smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"Welcome to the 'PURSUE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed Ren’s hand. 

"Let's go save the boy," she whispered. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Act 1 was over. The Pursuit had begun.
