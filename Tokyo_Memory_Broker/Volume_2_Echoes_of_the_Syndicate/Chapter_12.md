# Chapter 12: The Shard of Glass

The "Archive-Vault" was a cathedral of lead-glass and a smell of synthetic salt-water and sterilized gold. It sat deep within the Kanda Tower, a room filled with a dozen massive, clinical-white server racks that store the Syndicate’s most classified, high-grade data. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the vault, his charcoal suit whipping in the wind. Beside him, Hana was breathing in shallow, rhythmic bursts, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue as she scanned the internal resonance of the tower. 

In the center of the vault, suspended in a magnetic cradle, was a single obsidian sphere. It was the size of a human head, and it pulsed with a soft, bioluminescent blue. 

The Mind of the Syndicate. 

Ren moved toward the sphere, his hands steady. He could feel the Loom’s reach—not as a tech-grid, but as a "Linguistics-Field." It was trying to speak to him, its thoughts a rhythmic, violent deluge that bypassed his neurons and hit his soul. 

"Yield, Sato-san," a voice said, sounding like a thousands of voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"I won't yield," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at the floor. In the center of the vault was a single tattered print—a physical photo, identical to the one Hana had. 

"September 12, 2084," Ren whispered, his eyes fixing on the photo. 

Suddenly, his hand brushed against a small, fragmented Shard that had been hidden beneath the reader. It wasn't an "Hero" Shard. It wasn't an "Archive" Shard. It was a "Black-Project" Shard—ancient and flickering with a pale, ghostly light. 

Ren picked it up. 

The image that flickered to life on the grainy CRT monitor wasn't the penthouse. It wasn't the gala. It was a rain-slicked village in the Electrical Deadzone—a place that didn't exist anymore. 

In the center of the village stood a man in a tailored charcoal suit. 

It was Director Sato. 

He wasn't handing out Shards. He wasn't smiling. he was standing at the edge of a massive, clinical-white server rack that was geysering sparks and blue electricity into the mud. 

Around him, a dozen "Hollowed-Resonance" units were running through the village, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to the flickering blue vines of the "Sync-Grid." 

"Quiet-Death," Director Sato said, his voice a dry rasp that sounded exactly like Ren’s voice from the memory. 

"The promotion is complete. The city doesn't need a history. It needs a 'Blank-Slate' for the next 'Recorded Future'." 

He raised his resonance stave. 

"Delete them," Sato ordered, his voice sounding like a threat. 

The village turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Ren felt the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"No!" Ren cried, his hand slamming the Shard into the reader. 

"The Shard of Glass was a lie!" he screamed, his lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"It's not a lie, Ren," a voice smoothed into his brain—not Tanaka’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"It's a 'Recorded Future.' And you? You're the one who signed the contract." 

Ren looked at the screen. He looked at the man—the synth-bio ghost—and for the first time in Volume 2, he felt a genuine, unedited emotion. 

He felt *grief*. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his hand going to the siler-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes the city." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the vault flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHITECT-ERROR' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Ren Sato wasn't breathing. But he was finally awake. 
The Shard of Glass was over. The Pursuit had begun.
