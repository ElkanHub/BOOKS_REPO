# Chapter 24: The Siege of Kanda

The "Siege of Kanda" wasn't a military operation; it was a riot of unedited history and the smell of ancient ozone. It sat at the base of the Kanda Tower, the exact point where the city’s emergency lights and the Syndicate’s primary sensor-grid geysered into the dark. 

Hana stood in the center of the plaza, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, malevolent blue. Around her, a dozen hundreds of Hollowed-Resonance units were fighting the Syndicate’s Easers. These weren't the "Static-Headed" addicts anymore. These were "Truth-Wave" rioters—donors who had just seen the orphanage harvest and had decided that the Loom’s "Perfect Symmetry" was a cage they were willing to die to shatter. 

"Ren! Ren, it's working!" Hana screamed into her comm-link. 

But her link was dead. The "Grey Pulse" had scrambled all unlicensed hardware, turning the city into a single, collective migraine of unedited logic. 

Suddenly, a door in the tower’s maintenance hatch hissed open. 

It wasn't an Enforcer. It wasn't Tanaka. It was Taku. 

The boy in the clinical-white Syndicate uniform stood at the edge of the service ledge, his face impeccably manicured, his eyes clear of the white-noise cataracts. In his hand was a resonance stave, its magnetic spike geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain. 

"Hana-chan," Taku said, his voice a dry rasp that sounded exactly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

"The promotion is complete. The Archive is waiting for its 'Asset-Archivist.' Why are you still fighting for a history that doesn't exist?" 

Hana stopped. Her breath caught in her throat. 

"Taku, it's me! I'm your sister!" she screamed, her hand geysering sparks and blue electricity from her Siphon. 

The boy didn't answer. He didn't even look at her. He looked at the rioters, his eyes scanning the "Revision-Cloud" with a professional, detached logic. 

"Asset #409 is unauthorized," Taku said, his smile a brilliant, clinical white. 

"Yield, Girl. The 'Recorded Future' says you’re the one who deletes the city." 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"I'd rather have no history than a history written by you, Taku," Hana hissed, her voice sounding like a threat. 

She lunged. 

She didn't use her weapon. She used the momentum of her own flight jacket, slamming into the boy with the force of a mag-lev collision. The boy’s uniform geysered sparks and blue electricity, his magnetic shielding failing under the weight of Hana’s pulse. 

"Taku! Taku, please!" she cried, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

The boy didn't open his eyes. He "Synced." 

Suddenly, a door in the server-pillar behind him hissed open. 

It wasn't a clone. It was Tanaka. 

The Director’s face filled the sky, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white, his professional clipboard geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain. 

"The Siege of Kanda is over, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice roared from the ruins of a thousand billing-columns. 

"The boy is already part of the Loom. He’s the Archive’s shadow now. And you? You're the one who watched him go." 

Hana didn't yielding. 

Instead, she grabbed Taku’s hand. 

"Save the city, Taku!" she screamed, her voice a dry rasp. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Siege of Kanda was over. The Resolve had begun.
