# Chapter 28: The Broken Mirror

The "Broken Mirror" was a cathedral of high-frequency white-noise and a smell of ozone and synthetic jasmine. It sat in the ruins of the Core-Archives, the exact point where the city’s emergency lights and the Syndicate’s primary sensor-grid geysered into the dark. 

Hana stood in the center of the room, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen people were lying on the floor, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to the flickering blue vines of the "Sync-Grid." 

They weren't "Sync-Slaves" anymore. They were "Debt-Wiped" donors—people who had just felt the city’s collective trauma and had decided that the Loom’s "Perfect Symmetry" was a burden they were willing to share. 

Hana moved through the ward, her feet crunching on the glass of the shattered stasis-tanks. 

She wasn't looking for a savior. She was looking for Ren. 

In the center of the hangar, slumped against the master-control reader, sat a man in a matte-black suit. 

His skin was pale, mapped with the silver scars of a dozen extractions. His head lolled to the side, his eyes roll back in his head, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the mud. 

Ren. 

Hana ran to him, her hand touching the clinical white of his own port. 

"Ren! Ren, it's okay! The system... the system is 'Drafting' a new future!" 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of rubble. 

He opened his eyes. They were clear, the diamond-like brilliance gone. But they weren't the Director’s eyes—smooth, professional, and dripping with a malevolent confidence. They were the Broker’s eyes—dry, cynical, and full of a horrific, unedited emotion. 

They were full of *pain*. 

"The promotion is complete, Hana," Ren whispered, his voice sounding suddenly like a prayer. 

"I'm initiating a 'Final Audit' of Asset #409. Protocol: REBIRTH." 

"Ren, no! The 'Debt-Wipe' is overloading your core! If you don't get out, you’re going to be 'Integrated' forever!" 

Ren smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"The Broker doesn't take a commission, Hana," he whispered, his eyes fixing on the screen. 

"He just wipes the debt. And my debt? My debt was the 'Hero-Template'." 

Suddenly, a door in the server-pillar behind him hissed open. 

It wasn't an Enforcer. It was Taku. 

The boy in the clinical-white Syndicate uniform stood at the edge of the service ledge, his face impeccably manicured, his eyes clear of the white-noise cataracts. 

"Taku?" Hana whispered, her hand reaching for him. 

The boy didn't answer. He didn't even look at her. He looked at Ren—at the man with the sharp, intelligent face and the lopsided smile. 

"Asset #112 is unauthorized," Taku said, his voice a dry rasp that sounded suddenly like his own voice from the photo. 

"Yield,佐藤さん. The 'Recorded Future' says you’re the one who saves the city." 

He didn't raise his resonance stave. He raised his hand. 

In his hand was a small, tattered print—a physical photo, identical to the one Hana had. 

"September 12, 2084," Taku whispered, his eyes filling with a horrific, unedited emotion. 

"September 12th... that's the day we were happy, wasn't it?" 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"Taku!" she cried, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

The boy didn't yielding. 

He grabbed Hana’s hand. 

"Welcome to the 'FINAL AUDIT' phase, Hana-chan," Taku smiled, his lopsided expression matching the photo. 

Ren Sato looked at the boy. He looked at Hana. 

"Save the city, Hana," he whispered, his voice sounding like a threat. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Broken Mirror was over. The Resolve had begun.
