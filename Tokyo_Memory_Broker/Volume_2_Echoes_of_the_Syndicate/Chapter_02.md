# Chapter 02: The Siren Song

The "Hollowed-Resonance" camp was a cathedral of low-frequency static and a smell of ancient ozone. It was hidden beneath the ruins of the Shibuya mag-lev station, a maze of rusted tracks and flickering blue server-pillars that served as the heartbeat of the resistance. 

Hana sat on a crate in the center of the camp, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen people were arguing—not about the Syndicate’s security or the Grey Zone’s harvest, but about the message on the billboard. 

"The Amnesty Order, Hana!" a man named Jiro cried, his hand shaking. "It says we can have our *original* lives back. No harvest. No 'Drafting.' Just the memory of who we were before the Loom." 

Jiro was a "Fresh-Donor"—a man who had been harvested only a week before the Tower fell. He still remembered the smell of his daughter’s hair, but the image of her face was a dark, brooding violet. 

"It's a trap, Jiro!" Hana hissed, her eyes fixed on her weapon. "Tanaka isn't giving you anything. He's just 'Recalling' the templates. He's trying to rebuild the Master-Loom by using our own desperation as his starting data." 

"Then let him!" Jiro roared. "I'd rather be a 'Sync-Slave' and remember my daughter's lopsided smile than live in the dark with you." 

The camp went silent. The argument didn't end; it just died. 

Hana looked at the people around her. They weren't revolutionaries anymore. They were "Static-Heads"—addicts who were starving for the single, brilliant Shard of their own history. 

Suddenly, a hand touched her shoulder. 

It was Taku, her brother. He looked exactly like the boy in the photo—sharp, intelligent face, lopsided smile. But his eyes were clear of the white-noise cataracts. He was one of the few who had survived the Loom’s deactivation without losing his mind. 

"He's right, Hana," Taku whispered, his voice sounding suddenly like a prayer. 

"The silence is... it's too loud. I can't remember the color of our mother’s eyes. I can't remember the sound of the ocean in Kamakura." 

"Taku," Hana said, her voice sounding like a threat. 

"I'm going, Hana," Taku said, his hand going to the silver-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"The Amnesty Order is for 'Asset-Grade' donors. That's me. That's *you*." 

He pulled a small, tattered print from his pocket—a physical photo, identical to the one Hana had. 

"September 12, 2084," Taku whispered. "That's when we were happy, wasn't it?" 

Hana looked at the photo. She looked at Taku—at the boy who was her only anchor in the Grey Zone. 

"I'm going to the Kanda Tower, Hana. I'm going to get our past back." 

"Taku, wait!" Hana cried, standing up. 

But it was too late. He was already walking toward the entrance of the camp, his hand reaching for the first "Neutralizer" Shard on the door. 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the camp. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of Loom-data designed to scramble unlicensed hardware and overload neural ports. 

The server-pillars flickered and died. The camp’s emergency lights turned an angry red. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'AMNESTY-LINK' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-FEED ESTABLISHED.* 

"Welcome to the 'SIREN SONG' phase, Hana-chan," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed her Siphon and ran toward the door. 

"Taku!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

But the boy was gone. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost. 
The Siren Song had begun. And the Hollowed were already dancing.
"Welcome to the 'PURSUE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence.
