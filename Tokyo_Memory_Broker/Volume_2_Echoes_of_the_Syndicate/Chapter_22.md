# Chapter 22: The Dark Point

The "Dark Point" was a cathedral of high-frequency white-noise and a smell of smoke and ozone. It sat at the intersection of Shibuya and Shinjuku, the exact point where the city’s emergency lights and the Syndicate’s primary sensor-grid geysered into the dark. 

Hana stood in the center of the intersection, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen "Hollowed-Resonance" units were fighting the Syndicate’s Easers, but these weren't soldiers. They were rioters—donors who had just seen the orphanage harvest and had decided that the Loom’s "Perfect Symmetry" was a cage they were willing to die to shatter. 

"Ren! Ren, it's a trap!" Hana screamed into her comm-link. 

But her link was dead. The "Grey Pulse" had scrambled all unlicensed hardware, turning the city into a single, collective migraine of unedited logic. 

Suddenly, a door in the server-pillar hissed open. 

It wasn't Taku. It wasn't an Enforcer. It was a massive, holographic projection of Tanaka. 

The Director’s face filled the sky, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white, his professional clipboard geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain. 

"The promotion is complete, Neo-Tokyo," Tanaka’s voice roared from the ruins of a thousand billing-columns. 

"You've seen the cost. You've seen the 'Quiet-Death' of the orphanage. And now? Now the city is finally free of its own history." 

He didn't raise his resonance stave. He raised his hand. 

"Audit the debts," Tanaka ordered, his voice sounding like a threat. 

In the center of the intersection, a dozen donors fell to their knees. Their eyes turned a dark, bruised violet. They weren't rioters anymore. They were "Sync-Slaves"—a dozen biological nodes in the Loom’s new architecture. 

"No!" Hana cried, her hand geysering sparks and blue electricity from her Siphon. 

She wasn't a hero. She was a donor. 

"Hana-chan," Tanaka’s voice smoothed into her brain—not a broadcast, but a live, direct neural-link. 

"Look at the screen. Look at the boy." 

In the center of the holographic projection, Taku was standing at the edge of the Kanda Tower’s Service-Plinth. He was wearing the clinical-white Syndicate uniform, his face impeccably manicured, his eyes clear of the white-noise cataracts. 

"Taku!" Hana screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"Yield, Girl," Tanaka smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"The past is a debt that must be paid in blood, Director. And your 'Hero'? He's the one who signed the contract." 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the city. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of Loom-data designed to scramble unlicensed hardware and overload neural ports. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

"Welcome to the 'BROKER'S CHOICE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed her weapon. 

"Taku!" she screamed, her voice a dry rasp that sounded exactly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

"Save the city! You have to save the city!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Act 2B was over. The resolve had begun.
