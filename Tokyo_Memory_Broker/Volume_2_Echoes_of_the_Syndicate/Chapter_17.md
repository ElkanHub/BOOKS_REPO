# Chapter 17: The First Betrayal

The "First Betrayal" wasn't a gunshot or a data-spike. It was a silence. 

Hana stood in the center of the Archive-Hangar, her flight jacket zipped tight against the clinical, high-frequency white-noise of the tower. Opposite her, Ren Sato was a blur of charcoal silk and tattered, synth-bio skin. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of data. 

"The promotion is complete, Hana," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

"I'm going to the 'Processing Ward'. I'm going to find Taku. I'm going to save the boy." 

Hana didn't answer. She was staring at her weapon—her Siphon, its magnetic spike geysering sparks and blue electricity. 

"No," Hana whispered, her voice sounding like a threat. 

"You're not going anywhere, Ren Sato. Or Director Sato. Or whatever 'Template' Tanaka is running today." 

Ren stopped. His eyes were clear, the diamond-like brilliance gone. He looked exhausted. He looked like a man who had just used a muscle he didn't know he had. 

"Hana, I didn't... I didn't remember! The Hero-Template was a gift! It was a lie!" 

"That's the point, isn't it?" Hana spat, her eyes fixing on him. 

"You didn't 'remember' because it didn't happen. You're a synth-bio ghost, Ren. You're the piece of code the Syndicate uses to rebuild the city every time the data-core fails. You don't have a heart because you don't have a soul." 

She stepped closer, her finger touching the silver-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"The boy in the photo? That was Taku. And the man in the photo? That was the Director. You didn't save us, Ren. You *integrated* us." 

Hana felt the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"I'd rather have no history than a history written by you," she hissed, her voice sounding suddenly sharp. 

"I'm going to find Taku myself. I'm going to save my brother from the future YOU wrote." 

"Hana, wait!" Ren cried, his hand reaching for her. 

Hana didn't yield. 

Instead, she grabbed her Siphon and slammed the Sync-Lever of the communal reader. 

"Welcome to the 'SUB-BASEMENTS' phase, Sato-san," she smiled, her lopsided expression matching the photo. 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the hangar. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of Loom-data designed to scramble unlicensed hardware and overload neural ports. 

The server-pillars flickered and died. The hangar’s emergency lights turned an angry red. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHITECT-ERROR' ENGAGED. ASSET #1 VERIFIED.* 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase,佐藤さん," Tanaka’s voice smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She turned and ran toward the door. 

"Taku!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

But the boy was gone. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost. 
The First Betrayal was over. The Unravelling had begun.
