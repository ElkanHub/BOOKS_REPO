# Chapter 06: The Dead Hangar

The "Maintenance-Hatch" was a jagged, light-swallowing crack in the tower’s Null-Paint. It led directly into the Service-Plinth—the massive, unlisted sub-structure that held the tower’s power-cells and cooling-grids. 

Ren Sato led the way, his hands steady despite the rhythmic pulsing in his spinal port. Beside him, Hana was breathing in shallow, rhythmic bursts, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue as she scanned the internal resonance of the tower. 

"The tower isn't just a link, Ren," she whispered as they emerged into a massive, pressurized hangar. "It’s a 'Refiner.' It’s where the raw trauma of the Grey Zone is filtered into 'Loom-Grade' data." 

Hana pointed toward the center of the hangar, beneath a massive, glowing pillar made of a thousand server racks. 

In the center of the pillar, suspended in a magnetic cradle, was a single obsidian shard. It was the size of a human head, and it pulsed with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

The Dead Hangar. 

Ren moved with a fluid, mechanical grace that felt more like the Director’s than his own. He walked through the hangar, his hands outstretched, his eyes fixing on the server racks. 

Suddenly, a sharp, cold sensation hit his ear. His comm-link buzzed into life—not the Syndicate channel, but a private frequency he didn't recognize. 

"Yield, Broker 4-0-9," a voice said, sounding like a thousands of voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"I won't yield," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at the floor. In the center of the hangar was a massive pile of discarded Shards. They weren't glowing with a soft, bioluminescent blue. They were clear, without a single flicker of data. 

"Empty," Ren whispered, his hand going to one of the shards. 

"They're cleaning the past, Hana," he hissed, his eyes fixing on the Master-Loom. 

"The Syndicate isn't just harvesting the memories of the donors. They’re deleting the very possibility of the world’s history. They’re turning the city into a 'Blank-Slate' for the next 'Recorded Future'." 

He looked at Hana. She was crying—not for herself, but for her people. 

"Taku is already here, Hana," Ren said, his voice sounding like a promise. 

"If we don't turn the system back on, we can't save him. We can't save anyone." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the hangar flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHITECT-ERROR' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

"Welcome to the 'GATEKEEPER' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed her Siphon and ran toward the door. 

"Taku!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

But the boy was gone. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost. 
The Dead Hangar was over. The Pursuit had begun.
