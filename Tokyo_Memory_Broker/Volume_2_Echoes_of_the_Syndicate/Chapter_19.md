# Chapter 19: The Archive War

The "Processing Ward" was a cathedral of clinical white and a smell of synthetic jasmine and sterilized gold. It sat deep within the Kanda Tower, a room filled with a dozen massive, clinical-white server racks that monitored the Syndicate’s most classified "In-Processing" assets. 

Hana stood in the center of the ward, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, malevolent blue. Around her, a dozen people were lying in the tanks, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to the flickering blue vines of the "Sync-Grid." 

She wasn't looking for a savior anymore. She was looking for a ghost. 

"The tower isn't just a link, Hana-chan," Tanaka’s voice smoothed into her brain—not a recording, but a live, direct neural-link. 

"It's a 'Factory.' We don't just harvest the memories of our donors; we harvest the very possibility of their loyalty. And Taku? Taku was the perfect 'Asset.' He already had the 'Loom-Symmetry' in his blood." 

Hana didn't answer. She was busy scanning the "Revision-Cloud"—the place where the Syndicate stored the loyalty-scripts they were using to turn the Hollowed into its new Enforcers. 

She began to hack the peripheral files, her fingers moving with a fluid, mechanical grace that felt more like the Broker’s than her own. She was looking for her brother, but what she found was something else. 

She found the "Enforcer-Script"—the template that added the "Sync-Protocol" to a donor’s raw memory. 

*CLIENT #112: ARISAWA, T. (Active)* 
*PROTOCOL: REBIRTH (Loyalty Script v4.09)* 
*ROLE: ARCHIVE GUARD.* 

"Taku!" Hana cried, her hand slamming her Siphon into the server racks. 

Suddenly, a door in the server-pillar hissed open. 

It wasn't a clone. It was an Enforcer—but he wasn't wearing the matte-black suit of the Easers. He was wearing a clinical-white Syndicate uniform, his face impeccably manicured, his eyes clear of the white-noise cataracts. 

It was Taku. 

Hana stopped. Her breath caught in her throat. 

"Taku?" she whispered, her hand reaching for him. 

The boy didn't answer. He didn't even look at her. He looked at the terminal screen, his eyes scanning the "Revision-Cloud" with a professional, detached logic. 

"Asset #409 is unauthorized," Taku said, his voice a dry rasp that sounded exactly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

"Yield, Girl. The 'Recorded Future' says you’re the one who deletes the city." 

"Taku, it's me! It's Hana! It's your sister!" 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"Sister?" Taku asked, his head lolling to the side in a silent, agonizing roar. 

"No," he laughed, a wet, rattling sound that made Hana think of a dying radio. 

"The 'Recorded Future' doesn't have room for sisters. Tanaka says I’m the 'Asset-Archivist.' I’m the one who makes sure the 'Architect-Templates' don't get deleted before the next 'Draft' begins." 

He raised his resonance stave. 

"Yield, Hana-chan," Taku smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE WAR' phase." 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the ward. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of Loom-data designed to scramble unlicensed hardware and overload neural ports. 

The server-pillars flickered and died. The ward’s emergency lights turned an angry red. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ASSET-RECOVERY' ENGAGED. ASSET #112 VERIFIED.* 

Hana didn't yielding. 

Instead, she grabbed her Siphon and slammed it into Taku’s resonance stave. 

"Ren!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"He's here, Ren! He's an Enforcer! He's the 'Archive-Guard'!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Archive War was over. The Pursuit had reached its limit.
