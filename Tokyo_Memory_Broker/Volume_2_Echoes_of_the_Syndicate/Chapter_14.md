# Chapter 14: The Hidden Feed

The "Hidden-Feed" was a cathedral of high-frequency white-noise and a smell of ancient ozone and sterilized gold. It sat deep within the Processing Ward, a room filled with a dozen massive, clinical-white server racks that monitored the Syndicate’s most classified "In-Processing" assets. 

Hana stood in the center of the room, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, malevolent blue. Beside her, the "Ghost-Hana" was exploring the "Hero-Vault," her mind already integrated into the Syndicate’s polished narratives. 

"Ren... he's in the 'Archive-Vault', Hana," the holographic girl whispered, her eyes fixing on the terminal screen. 

"He’s already found the 'Black-Project' Shard. He’s already seen the man he used to be." 

Hana didn't answer. She was already busy scanning the "Revision-Cloud"—the place where the Syndicate stored the raw, unedited master-feeds. 

She began to hack the peripheral files, her fingers moving with a fluid, mechanical grace that felt more like the Broker’s than her own. She was looking for Taku, but what she found was something else. 

She found the "Architect-Feed"—the place where the Syndicate monitored its dozens of clones.

"The tower isn't just a link, Hana-chan," Tanaka’s voice smoothed into her brain—not a recording, but a live, direct neural-link. 

"It's a 'Factory.' We don't just harvest the memories of our donors; we harvest the very possibility of their loyalty. We rebuild the city every time the data-core fails." 

He began to broadcast a new image on the grainy CRT monitor. 

It was a clinical white room, filled with a dozen small, clinical-white beds. 

An orphanage. 

In the center of the room stood a man in a tailored charcoal suit. 

It was Director Sato. 

He didn't raise his resonance stave. He wasn't smiling. He was holding a clipboard, his face an impeccably manicured mask of clinical detachedness. 

"Record of Harvest: October 12, 2084," Sato said, his voice a dry rasp that sounded exactly like Ren’s voice from the memory. 

Around him, a dozen "Hollowed-Resonance" units were moving through the beds, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to the flickering blue vines of the "Sync-Grid." 

The children weren't screaming. They weren't fighting. They were waiting. 

"Delete them," Sato ordered, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"The promotion is complete. The city doesn't need a history. It needs a 'Blank-Slate' for the next 'Recorded Future'." 

He raised his hand and pressed a button on the clipboard. 

Suddenly, the children’s eyes turned a dark, bruised violet. They weren't people anymore. They were "Sync-Slaves"—a dozen biological nodes in the Loom’s new architecture. 

"No!" Hana cried, her hand slamming her Siphon into the terminal. 

But it was too late. The feed was already over. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Hana-chan," Tanaka laughed, a wet, rattling sound. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed her Siphon and ran toward the door. 

"Ren!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"It wasn't a village, Ren! It was an orphanage! You deleted an orphanage!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Hidden Feed was over. The Pursuit had begun.
