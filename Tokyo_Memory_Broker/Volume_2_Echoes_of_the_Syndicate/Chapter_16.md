# Chapter 16: The Void Inside

The "Void" was a cathedral of silence and a smell of synthetic salt-water and sterilized gold. It sat deep within the Core-Archives, a room encased in a single, massive block of lead-glass that shielded it from the Syndicate’s primary sensor-grid. 

Ren Sato sat on the floor, his charcoal suit shredded, his skin pale and cold. Above him, the rain was a relentless grey curtain, but it no longer tasted of ozone. The high-frequency hum that had defined his world for a decade was gone. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of rubble. 

He reached for his chest, his hand searching for the rhythmic, comforting beat of a human heart. 

He didn't find one. 

His chest didn't move. There was no pulse. No breath. 

"The Void Inside is a lie, Ren," a voice smoothed into his brain—not Tanaka’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"I don't need a fix," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

He was staring at the Shard of Glass—the "Black-Project" Shard that showed him ordering the "Quiet-Death" of the orphanage. 

"If I am that monster, who is the man who saved Hana?" he whispered, his eyes fixing on the screen. 

"The man who saved her was a 'Hero-Template', Ren," the Loom whispered, its thoughts a flicker of white-noise. 

"He wasn't real. He was a piece of code Tanaka gave you to keep the Architect from 'Drowning.' He was the 'Gift' that allowed you to pretend that the harvest had a purpose." 

Ren felt the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his hand going to the siler-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes the city." 

Suddenly, a door in the server-pillar hissed open. 

It wasn't Tanaka. It wasn't an Enforcer. It was Hana. 

She looked exactly like the girl in the photo, her flight jacket zipped tight against the cold. But her eyes weren't clear of the white-noise cataracts. They were full of a horrific, unedited emotion. 

They were full of *disgust*. 

"Hana..." Ren whispered, his hand reaching for her. 

She didn't take it. She stepped back, her hand going to her weapon. 

"I found it, Ren. The 'Draft' files. I saw the orphans. I saw the faces of the children you 'Deleted'." 

She looked at him—at the man with the sharp, intelligent face and the lopsided smile. 

"You're not a hero, Ren. You're not even a man. You're a 'Template.' A piece of Syndicate hardware that’s been re-rolled so many times you don't even remember your own blood." 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"Hana, wait! I didn't... I don't remember!" 

"That's the point, isn't it?" Hana spat, her voice sounding like a threat. 

"You don't remember the cost. You just remember the 'Hero-Loop.' I'd rather have no history than a history written by you." 

She turned and ran toward the door. 

"Hana!" Ren screamed, his lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

But she didn't look back. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost. 
The Void Inside was over. The Unravelling had begun.
