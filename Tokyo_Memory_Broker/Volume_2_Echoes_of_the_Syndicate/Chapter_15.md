# Chapter 15: The Archive's Lie

The "Midpoint" of the Kanda Tower was a cathedral of lead-glass and a smell of synthetic jasmine and sterilized gold. It sat between the Core-Archives and the Processing Ward, a room encased in a single, massive block of lead-glass that reflected the flickering, rhythmic red of the city’s emergency lights. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the room, his charcoal suit whipping in the wind. Beside him, Hana was breathing in shallow, rhythmic bursts, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, malevolent blue as she scanned the terminal screen. 

"Ren! Ren, look at this!" 

She didn't use her weapon. She used her fingers, pulling the "Hidden-Feed" video onto the master-monitor. 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

He watched the screen. 

He saw himself—no, he saw Director Sato. The man in the tailored suit with the professional, detached clipboard. He saw the orphanage. He saw the children, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to the flickering blue vines of the "Sync-Grid." 

"Delete them," Sato ordered. 

The image geysered sparks and high-frequency white-noise, but the Director’s voice didn't flicker. It was calm. It was certain. 

Ren felt the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"The orphanage was an error, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed into his brain—not a recording, but a live, direct neural-link. 

"But you're the one who signed the contract. You were the one who told me that a single child’s potential for loyalty was worth more than a dozen 'Blank-Slate' memories." 

Ren didn't yield. He "Synced." 

Suddenly, he felt a sharp, cold sensation hit his ear. His comm-link buzzed into life—not the Syndicate channel, but a private frequency he didn't recognize. 

"It's true, Ren," the Director’s voice whispered, sounding like his own voice from the memory. 

"I wasn't reset as punishment. I was reset because I was 'Drowning.' I was starting to care about the donors. I was starting to care about Arisawa's girl. You... you were the piece of code I created to keep the 'Architect-Template' from stagnating." 

Ren looked at the screen. He looked at the man—the synth-bio ghost—and for the first time in Volume 2, he felt a genuine, unedited emotion. 

He felt *horror*. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his hand going to his own spinal port. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes the city." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the room flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHITECT-RECOVERY' ENGAGED. ASSET #1 VERIFIED.* 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed into the silence. 

"You weren't reset to be a Broker, Ren. You were 'Re-Rolled.' You were the piece of code we keep in the dark, just in case the system needs to remember its own cost." 

He reached for Ren’s hand. 

"But now? Now the system is hungry. And the Director is waiting for his 'Hero' to return." 

Ren lunged. 

He didn't use the Spike-Knife. He used the momentum of his own synth-bio body, slamming into the master-monitor with the force of a mag-lev collision. 

The screen shattered. The "Hidden-Feed" geysered sparks and blue electricity from the server racks. 

Ren fell to the ground, his breath sounding like a death rattle. The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

"The Archive's Lie is over, Sato-san," Tanaka laughed, a wet, rattling sound. 

"Welcome to the 'UNRAVEL' phase." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Ren Sato wasn't breathing. But he was finally awake. 
Act 2A was over. The Pursuit had reached its limit.
