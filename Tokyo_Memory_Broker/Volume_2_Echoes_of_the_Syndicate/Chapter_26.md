# Chapter 26: The Archivist's Stand

The "Archivist's Stand" was a cathedral of high-frequency white-noise and a smell of ancient ozone and sterilized gold. It sat at the very top of the Kanda Tower, a room encased in a single, massive block of lead-glass that overlooked the ruins of Shibuya and the flickering, rhythmic red of the city’s emergency lights. 

Ren Sato stood at the edge of the service-ledge, his charcoal suit turned a deep, matte-black. His hand was on his weapon, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of data. 

In the center of the ledge stood Archivist Kyo. 

He didn't raise his resonance stave. He raised his hand. 

In his hand was a single, obsidian sphere. It was the size of a human head, and it pulsed with a soft, bioluminescent blue. 

The Mind of the Archive. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," Kyo smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"The city is waiting for its 'Asset-Archivist.' Why are you still fighting for a history that doesn't exist? Why turn the Loom off when you can turn it *on*?" 

Ren didn't answer. He was staring at the man—the synth-bio ghost who had been his "Shadow." 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes you," he smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"Yield, Sato-san!" Kyo's voice geysered sparks and high-frequency white-noise from his own spinal port. 

He lunged. 

He didn't use a resonance stave. He used the momentum of his own tech-grid, his hand reaching for the silver-ringed port at the base of Ren’s own skull. 

Ren Sato didn't yield. He "Synced." 

He moved with a fluid, mechanical grace that felt more like the Director’s than his own. He was fighting himself, literally. He saw his own lopsided smile on Kyo’s face. He felt his own "Primal-Code" in every data-pulse. 

"Who are you, Kyo?" Ren cried, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"I'm the 'Archivist'!" Kyo roared, his face filling with a horrific, unedited emotion. 

"I'm the one who makes sure the 'Architect-Templates' don't get deleted! I'm the one who curates the 'Hero-Vault'! I'm the one who knows the cost of the harvest!" 

He laughed, a wet, rattling sound that made Ren think of a dying radio. 

"The Archive's Stand is a lie, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed into his brain—not a broadcast, but a live, direct neural-link. 

"But you're the one who signed the contract." 

Suddenly, a door in the server-pillar behind Kyo hissed open. 

It wasn't a clone. It was Tanaka. 

The Director’s face fill the sky, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white, his professional clipboard geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain. 

"Kyo isn't a person, Sato-san," Tanaka smiled, his voice sounding suddenly like a prayer. 

"He's a 'Backup.' He's the piece of code I created to keep the 'Architect-Template' from stagnating. He's the one who remembers the cost of the harvest so the Director doesn't have to." 

Ren looked at Kyo. He looked at the man—the synth-bio ghost—and for the first time in Volume 2, he felt a genuine, unedited emotion. 

He felt *pity*. 

"You're just another 'Re-Roll', aren't you?" Ren whispered, his hand going to his head. 

"Yes," Kyo laughed. "And so are you. We're all 'Asset-Grade' memories, Sato-san. We're all just pieces of the Loom’s 'Perfect Symmetry'." 

Ren didn't yielding. 

Instead, he grabbed Kyo’s hand. 

"The Archive's Stand is over, Tanaka," Ren said, his voice sounding like a promise. 

"Welcome to the 'DEBT-WIPE' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Archivist's Stand was over. The Resolve had begun.
