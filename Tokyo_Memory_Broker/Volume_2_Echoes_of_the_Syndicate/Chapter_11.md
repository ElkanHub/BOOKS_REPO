# Chapter 11: The Archivist's Game

The "Processing Ward" was a cathedral of clinical white and a smell of synthetic jasmine and sterilized gold. It sat deep within the Kanda Tower, a room filled with a dozen massive, clinical-white tanks of stasis-fluid—but these weren't for "Architect" clones. 

They were for "Asset-Grade" donors. 

Hana stood in the center of the ward, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen people were lying in the tanks, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to the flickering blue vines of the "Sync-Grid." 

They weren't being erased. They were being "Rewritten." 

Hana moved through the ward, her fingers moving with a fluid, mechanical grace that felt more like the Broker’s than her own. She was looking for Taku, but what she found was something else. 

She found the "Revision-Cloud"—the place where the Syndicate stored the loyalty-scripts they were using to turn the Hollowed into its new Enforcers. 

"The Amnesty Order is for 'Asset-Grade' donors, Girl," a voice smoothed into her brain—not Tanaka’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"I don't need a fix," Hana whispered, her eyes fixed on the terminal screen. 

The screen was a maze of high-frequency white-noise and molten hardware. But beneath the noise, she could see the "Loom’s-Fingerprints"—the raw, unedited drafts of the world’s history. 

She found Taku’s file. 

*CLIENT #112: ARISAWA, T.* 
*STATUS: IN-PROCESSING.* 
*PROTOCOL: REBIRTH (Loyalty Script v4.09)* 

Hana opened the file. 

The image that flickered to life on the grainy CRT monitor wasn't the boy she knew. It was a man in a matte-black enforcer suit, his skin mapped with the silver scars of a thousand low-level extractions. His eyes were clear of the white-noise cataracts, but they were devoid of any human warmth. 

He was standing in a high-end Shinjuku office, his hand on the shoulder of a young girl with a lopsided smile. 

"Recorded on September 12, 2084," Hana whispered, her eyes fixing on the screen. 

"September 12th is the day we were happy," she cried, her hand slamming her Siphon into the server racks. 

"Is it?" the Loom asked. "Or is it the day the Architect wrote the ending you’re still trying to save?" 

She moved to the next tank. Inside was Taku. 

He looked peaceful. He looked... happy. 

"Taku!" Hana cried, her hand slamming into the glass wall of the tank. 

The boy didn't open his eyes. He "Synced." 

Suddenly, his eyes rolled back in his head, his skin turning a dark, bruised violet. He wasn't her brother anymore. He was a "Sync-Slave"—a biological node in the Loom’s new architecture. 

"No!" Hana screamed, her hand geysering sparks and blue electricity from the tank’s emergency release. 

But it wasn't enough. The reader was locked. 

"The Archivist's Game is over, Hana-chan," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"The boy is already part of the Loom. He’s the Archive’s shadow now." 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the ward. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of Loom-data designed to scramble unlicensed hardware and overload neural ports. 

The server-pillars flickered and died. The ward’s emergency lights turned an angry red. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ASSET-RECOVERY' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Girl," Tanaka smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed her Siphon and ran toward the door. 

"Ren!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"Save the boy! You have to save the boy!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Archivist's Game was over. The Pursuit had begun.
