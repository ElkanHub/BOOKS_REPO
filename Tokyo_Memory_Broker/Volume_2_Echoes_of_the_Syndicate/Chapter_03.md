# Chapter 03: The First Defection

The dawn in the Grey Zone wasn't a sunrise; it was a clinical, high-resolution broadcast of a future that didn't exist yet. 

Ren Sato stood on a service ledge a hundred feet above the Shibuya ruins, his charcoal suit whipping in the wind. Below him, a dozen "Hollowed-Resonance" units were walking toward the Shinjuku spire—not as soldiers, but as "Asset-Grade" donors. 

They weren't screaming. They weren't fighting. They were waiting. 

"The Amnesty Order is a lie, Ren," a voice whispered from his comm-link—not Hana’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"I don't need a fix," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his hand going to his own spinal port. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes the city," he smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

Ren looked at the people—at reality’s leftovers. They were carrying their portable readers, their tattered photos, their "Personal Anchors." They were carrying their stories into the Maw. 

Suddenly, a figure caught his eye. 

It was a boy in a faded flight jacket—Taku. He wasn't running. He was walking with a deliberate, rhythmic pace, his eyes clear of the white-noise cataracts. He looked exactly like the boy in the photo—sharp, intelligent face, lopsided smile. 

"Taku!" Ren cried, his voice sounding through the static. 

"Ren," Taku answered, his voice sounding suddenly like a prayer. 

"I'm going to the Kanda Tower, Ren. I'm going to get my past back. I'm going to find the memory of our mother’s eyes." 

Ren felt the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"The Amnesty Order is for 'Asset-Grade' donors, Taku!" Ren hissed, his hand going to the siler-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"Tanaka isn't giving you anything. He's just 'Recalling' the templates. He's trying to rebuild the Master-Loom by using your own desperation as his starting data." 

"Then let him!" Taku roared. "I'd rather be a 'Sync-Slave' and remember my mother's lopsided smile than live in the dark with you." 

The boy didn't look back. He walked through the gates of the Shinjuku spire, the magnetic shield geysering sparks and high-frequency noise. 

Suddenly, the red lights in the city flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'AMNESTY-RECIEPT' ENGAGED. ASSET #112 VERIFIED.* 

Ren Sato felt his heart—or the mechanical pump that served as his heart—racing. 

He looked at the gate. He looked at the boy—at the last piece of Hana’s world disappearing into the maw of the Syndicate. 

"He's going to save the city, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"And you? You're the one who watched him go." 

Ren didn't yield. 

Instead, he grabbed his Spike-Knife and dove through the drainage tunnel that led to the mag-lev tracks. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

The First Defection had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost. 
"Welcome to the 'PURSUE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 
Ren stood up, his face a mask of cold, focused determination. He didn't reach for the Diamond Shard. He reached for his weapon. 
The Siren Song was over. The Siege had begun.
