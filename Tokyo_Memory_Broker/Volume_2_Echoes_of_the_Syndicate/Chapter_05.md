# Chapter 05: Infiltration Prep

The "Safehouse" was a tomb of rusted chrome and the smell of ancient ozone. It sat beneath the ruins of the Shibuya mag-lev tracks, a room encased in a single, massive block of lead-glass that shielded it from the Syndicate’s primary sensor-grid. 

Hana sat on the edge of a crate, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Beside her, Ren Sato was already busy scanning the tower’s internal resonance, his hands steady despite the rhythmic pulsing in his spinal port. 

"The tower isn't just a link, Ren," Hana whispered, her eyes fixed on the holographic blueprint of the Kanda Tower. 

The blueprint was flickering, the structural lines turning from white to a rhythmic, angry red. 

"The security grid is still there. The Master-Loom... it’s not deactivated. It’s 'Hibernating.' It’s waiting for the system to find its Architect." 

Ren didn't answer. He was staring at the image of the "Gilded Cage"—the penthouse suite where he’d been imprisoned in Volume 1. 

"Taku is already in the 'Processing Ward'," Hana continued, her voice trembling. 

"He’s not being harvested, Ren. He's being 'Rewritten.' Tanaka is using the Amnesty Order to find the donors who have the highest 'Loom-Symmetry' and turning them into his new Enforcers." 

Ren stood up, his hand going to the Spike-Knife in his sleeve. 

"The Amnesty Order is for 'Asset-Grade' donors, Hana," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

"That's mean I’m the only one who can get him out." 

He gestured toward the screen. 

"The 'Director’s Access-Pipe' is still active. The Syndicate didn't change the codes because they didn't think I’d ever remember them. They didn't think a 'Deleted' man could ever come home." 

Hana looked at him. She looked at the man—the synth-bio ghost—and for the first time in Volume 2, she felt a genuine, unedited emotion. 

She felt *fear*. 

"It's a trap, Ren," she whispered. "Tanaka wants you to come back. He wants the Architect to turn the system on." 

"I'm not going back to turn it on, Hana," Ren said, his voice sounding like a promise. 

"I'm going back to 'Audit' the records. If I can get to the 'Core-Archives,' I can delete Taku’s template before the sync is complete. I can wipe the boy’s debt." 

He reached for her hand. 

"But I need the Siphon. I need the girl who remembers." 

Hana didn't yield. 

Instead, she grabbed her Siphon and slammed the Sync-Lever of the communal reader. 

"Welcome to the 'INFILTRATION-PREP' phase, Sato-san," she smiled, her lopsided expression matching the photo. 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the safehouse. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of Loom-data designed to scramble unlicensed hardware and overload neural ports. 

The server-pillars flickered and died. The camp’s emergency lights turned an angry red. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHITECT-RECOVERY' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

"Welcome to the 'PURSUE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed Ren’s hand. 

"Let's go save the boy," she whispered. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Infiltration was starting. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
