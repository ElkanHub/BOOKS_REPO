# Chapter 10: The Memory of Water

The "Sensory-Deprivation Tank" was a cathedral of bioluminescent blue and a smell of synthetic salt-water. It sat within the Core-Archives, a room encased in a single, massive block of lead-glass that shielded it from the Syndicate’s primary sensor-grid. 

Ren Sato lay in the tank, his body suspended in a rhythmic, violent energy. He wasn't breathing. He didn't have a heartbeat. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of data. 

Inside his brain, the world was changing. 

He wasn't in the Kanda Tower anymore. He wasn't in the Grey Zone. He was in a high-end Shinjuku penthouse, his charcoal suit impeccably dry even in the damp caverns. He was drinking amber liquid from a glass that cost more than a dozen lives. 

Opposite him sat a man with a sharp, intelligent face and a lopsided smile. 

It was Dr. Arisawa. 

But he didn't look like the broken, dying man Ren had harvested in Akihabara. He looked healthy. He looked... happy. 

"The promotion is complete, Ren," Arisawa smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"We did it. The Loom is active. The city is finally free of its own history. Every soul in Neo-Tokyo can finally find a peaceful, edited end." 

Ren looked at the glass. He could feel the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"It's a lie," a voice whispered from his comm-link—not Hana’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"I don't need a fix," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

"Is it a lie, Ren?" Arisawa asked, his hand going to his own spinal port. 

"Or is it the only way to save the Architect from the monsters he’d already created?" 

He gestured toward the window. 

Outside, the city wasn't a ruin. It was a paradise of neon and "Perfect Love" and "Eternal Youth." The people weren't screaming in the streets; they were walking hand-in-hand, their eyes clear, their hands reaching out to touch the rain. 

"September 12, 2084," Ren whispered. 

"Yes," Arisawa smiled. "Because today is the day the future begins. Today is the day the Loom’s 'Perfect Symmetry' becomes the only timeline." 

Ren felt the weight of the water. He could feel the rhythmic, violent energy of the tank, the bioluminescent blue seeping into his synth-bio skin. 

"You were a hero, Ren," Arisawa whispered, his voice sounding suddenly like a prayer. 

"You were the one who tried to stop the 'Records' from becoming a cage. You were the one who gave the donors a choice." 

Ren closed his eyes. 

He didn't remember the boardroom. He didn't remember the Akihabara "Grave-Zones." He only remembered the water. 

Suddenly, a sharp, cold sensation hit his ear. His comm-link buzzed into life—not the Syndicate channel, but a private frequency he didn't recognize. 

"Ren! Ren, get out!" 

It was Hana. Her voice was frantic, sounding through the static. 

"The tank is a 'Sync-Lock'! Tanaka is using the 'Memory of Water' to anchor your 'Hero-Template'! If you don't get out, you’re going to be 'Integrated' forever!" 

Ren Sato opened his eyes. 

The penthouse wasn't a paradise. It was a projection. 

Arisawa’s face began to flicker, his healthy skin turning to a dark, bruised violet. His brilliant white teeth turned to yellowed, Loom-residue-stained fangs. 

"Yield, Sato-san," the monster said, its voice sounding like a thousands of voices combined. 

Ren lunged. 

He didn't use the Spike-Knife. He used the momentum of his own synth-bio body, slamming into the glass wall of the tank with the force of a mag-lev collision. 

The glass shattered. The salt-water flooded the floor, geysering sparks and blue electricity from the server racks. 

Ren fell to the ground, his breath sounding like a death rattle. The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVIST'S GAME' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the ruins of a nearby reader. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Ren Sato wasn't breathing. But he was finally awake. 
The Memory of Water was over. The Pursuit had begun.
