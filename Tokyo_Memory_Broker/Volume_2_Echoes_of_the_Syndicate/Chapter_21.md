# Chapter 21: The Grey Pulse

The "Grey-Pulse" wasn't a broadcast; it was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited data that bypassed the Syndicate’s primary sensory-grid and hit the city’s collective consciousness. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the Old-Terminal, his charcoal suit turned a deep, matte-black. His hand was on the master-control lever, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the mud. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of truth. 

"The promotion is complete, Neo-Tokyo," Ren whispered, his voice sounding through the static. 

He didn't use his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at the "Draft-Archive" monitor. It showed the orphanage harvest—the clinical white room, the clinical white beds, the children with their eyes rolled back in their heads. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'DIRECTOR-OVERRIDE' ENGAGED.* 
*BROADCAST: UNFILTERED ARCHIVE FEED #409.* 

Ren slammed the lever. 

Suddenly, the world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The image of the orphanage didn't just appear on the billboards in Shibuya and Shinjuku. It appeared in the "Hero-Vaults" of the donors. It appeared in the "Personal Anchors" of the Hollowed. It appeared in the minds of the people who were currently standing in the Kanda Tower’s Processing Ward. 

"September 12, 2084," the Director’s voice smoothed—not from the terminal, but from a thousand voices combined. 

The people saw the Director’s professional clipboard. They saw the children’s eyes turning a dark, bruised violet. They saw the "Quiet-Death" of the city’s next generation. 

"No!" a voice roared from the ruins of a nearby reader—not Tanaka’s voice, but the collective, synthesized roar of a city that had just been woken from a "Hero-Loop." 

The "Loom-Symmetry" shattered. 

The high-frequency hum that had defined Neo-Tokyo for a decade geysered into a rhythmic, violent noise. The server-pillars throughout the city flickered and died. The "Amnesty Order" billboard in Shinjuku exploded in a shower of sparks and high-frequency white-noise. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'CIVIL-UNREST' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-FEED DISCONNECTED.* 

Ren Sato felt the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at him—at the man who had just shown them their own cost. 

"He's giving them the truth,佐藤さん," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"But the truth... the truth is a debt that must be paid in blood." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Ren stood up, his face a mask of cold, focused determination. 

The Grey Pulse had begun. And the rebellion was already dancing in the streets.
