# Chapter 06: The Neutralizer Hunt

The "Neutralizer Hunt" was a cathedral of rusted chrome and the smell of ancient ozone and rotting data. It sat deep within the "Sub-Basements" of the Kanda Tower, the only part of the monolith where the Null-Paint had failed, the surface of the walls mapped with the silver scars of a thousand low-level extractions. 

Ren Sato wandered through the "Trash-Heaps"—the place where the Syndicate dumped its failed memories, its "Static-Headed" asset-templates, and its unedited data-cores. 

He didn't have his Spike-Knife. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of rubble. 

He was looking for the "Filter-Nodes"—the original, '81-era server hardware that the Director had used to curate the first "Self-Death" experiments. Without them, the Echo-Cough would continue to "Symmetry" the city until every soul in Neo-Tokyo was a single, collective migraine. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," a voice said, sounding like his own voice from the memory. 

But it wasn't the Director’s voice—dry, cynical, and devoid of any human warmth. It was a recorded pulse, a "Primal-Code" left for the Architect in case the Tower’s AI ever turned against its masters. 

Ren didn't type. He "Synced." 

His hand brushed against a heavy, clinical-white server-pillar, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the mud. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'DEEP-ARCHIVE' ENGAGED.* 
*ACCESS CODE: THE-COST-OF-HISTORY* 
*WELCOME HOME, ARCHITECT #1.* 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

Suddenly, a door in the server-pillar hissed open. 

It wasn't a clone. It was an Enforcer—but he wasn't wearing the matte-black suit of the Easers. He was wearing a clinical-white Syndicate uniform, his face impeccably manicured, his eyes clear of the white-noise cataracts. 

It was "Asset-X." 

The boy in the clinical-white uniform stood at the edge of the service ledge, his face fixed in a professional, detached smile. In his hand was a resonance stave, its magnetic spike geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain. 

"The Neutralizer Hunt is a lie, Sato-san," Asset-X said, his voice a dry rasp that sounded exactly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

"There's no such thing as a 'Neutralizer'. The city is 'Drowning' because memory is the ultimate debt. And you? You're the one who signed the contract." 

Ren lunged. 

He didn't use a resonance stave. He used the momentum of his own synth-bio body, slamming into the boy with the force of a mag-lev collision. The boy’s uniform geysered sparks and blue electricity, his magnetic shielding failing under the weight of Ren’s pulse. 

"Who are you?" Ren cried, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"I'm the 'Archivist'!" Asset-X laughed, a wet, rattling sound that made Ren think of a dying radio. 

"I'm the piece of code Tanaka created to keep the 'Architect-Template' from stagnating. I'm the one who remembers the cost of the harvest so the Director doesn't have to." 

Asset-X looked at Ren—at the man with the sharp, intelligent face and the lopsided smile. 

"The promotion is over, Sato-san," Asset-X whispered, his eyes filling with a horrific, unedited emotion. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the sub-basement flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'DIRECTOR-OVERRIDE' ENGAGED. ASSET #1 VERIFIED.* 

Asset-X geysered into the dark, his form dissolving into a spray of blue electricity and clinical-white static. 

Ren Sato stood over the smoking remains of the server-pillar. In his hand was a single, obsidian sphere—the Filter-Node. 

He didn't open his eyes. He "Synced." 

"The Neutralizer Hunt is over, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and ancient logic. 

Ren didn't yielding. 

He grabbed the sphere and ran toward the door. 

"The Siege of Kanda is over, Hana!" he screamed into his comm-link. 

"Welcome to the 'ELECTION CALL' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Resolve had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
