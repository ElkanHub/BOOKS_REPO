# Chapter 02: The First Cough

The "Hollowed-Clinic" was a cathedral of low-frequency static and moving shadows. It sat beneath the ruins of the Shibuya mag-lev station, a room encased in a single, massive block of lead-glass that shielded the donors from the Syndicate’s primary sensor-grid. 

But the Syndicate was gone. The sensor-grid was empty. The clinic was just a room, and the donors weren't donors anymore. 

They were victims. 

Hana sat on a crate in the center of the clinic, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen people were lying on tattered blankets, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to a flickering blue server-pillar that looked like it had been scavenged from a dumpster. 

They weren't "Syncing" with the Loom. They were "Syncing" with the city. 

Suddenly, a man named Jiro slammed his hands against the floor. He wasn't screaming. He was coughing—a dry, rhythmic sound that geysered sparks and high-frequency white-noise from his mouth. 

"September 12, 2084," Jiro roared, his voice sounding like a thousand voices combined. 

"The orphanage harvest. I remember the smell of the jasmine. I remember the sound of the children’s laughter turning to static." 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He had the "Echo-Cough." 

Hana ran to him, her hand touching the clinical white of his own port. 

"Jiro, stop! It's a lie! The 'Recorded Future' is over!" 

Suddenly, a sharp, cold sensation hit Hana’s ear. Her own spinal port geysered sparks and blue electricity into the room. 

She wasn't a hero. She was a donor. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Inside her brain, the clinic wall vanished. She wasn't in Shibuya anymore. She was in a clinical white hallway in the Kanda Tower, her flight jacket replaced by a matte-black Enforcer suit. She was carrying a resonance stave, its magnetic spike geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

Opposite her stood a man in a tailored charcoal suit—Taku. 

"Yield, Girl," Taku said, his voice a dry rasp that sounded exactly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," he smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"It says I’m the one who saves the city." 

Hana lunged. 

She didn't use her weapon. She used the momentum of her own guilt, slamming into her brother with the force of a mag-lev collision. She felt the weight of his guilt, the rhythmic, violent energy of his "Archive-Guard" script. 

Suddenly, she was back in the clinic. 

Jiro was still coughing, his eyes rolled back in his head. But he wasn't alone. Another woman was coughing. Then a child. 

"The Echo-Cough is a 'Self-Repair' protocol, Sato-san," a voice smoothed into Hana’s brain—not Tanaka’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Grey Collective. 

"The Loom is trying to 'Symmetry' the city by making every donor experience the same pain. It’s trying to build a 'Perfect Future' out of the rubble of the Architect’s mistakes." 

Hana looked at the people. She looked at her own hands. They were steady, but they were the hands of a statue. 

"Ren!" she screamed into her comm-link. 

"Ren, it's not a virus! It's the Loom! It's trying to come home!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The First Cough was over. The Siege had begun.
