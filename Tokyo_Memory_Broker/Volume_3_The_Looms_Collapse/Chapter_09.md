# Chapter 09: The Ghost of Arisawa

The "Ghost-Msg" wasn't a video or a text-file. It was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited data that had been hidden in the clinical white noise of the tower’s peripheral archives. 

Hana stood in the center of the ruins of Dr. Arisawa’s old lab in Akihabara, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, malevolent blue. Around her, the "Electrical Deadzone" was alive. The copper trees were geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain, their leaves humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

She wasn't looking for a savior anymore. She was looking for a ghost. 

She reached for the "Arisawa-Cache"—a small, obsidian sphere that she had found buried beneath the ruins of the first server racks. 

Ren didn't have to "Sync" it. 

Hana "Synced." 

Suddenly, her brain geysered white-noise and high-frequency static. 

Inside her mind, the ruins of Akihabara vanished. She wasn't in the deadzone anymore. She was in a clinical white boardroom in the Kanda Tower, her flight jacket replaced by a tailored charcoal suit. Opposite her stood Dr. Arisawa. 

He didn't have a clipboard. He didn't have a white coat. He looked like a man who had just seen the "Recorded Future." 

"The promotion is complete, Child," Arisawa said, his voice sounding through the static. 

"If you're hearing this, it means the Loom has finally de-fragmented. It means my daughter is safe, and the Director is... well, the Director is exactly what we created him to be." 

Arisawa laughed, a wet, rattling sound that made Hana think of a dying radio. 

"The Loom wasn't a cage, Hana-chan," Arisawa whispered, his voice sounding suddenly like a prayer. 

" Tanaka didn't build it to harvest the donors’ memories. He built it to 'Filter' them. He built it because the raw, unedited history of this city is too much for a human mind to carry. He built the Loom because the truth is a debt that would delete us all." 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same boy. All hearing the same promise. 

"The 'Echo-Cough' is just the beginning," Arisawa said, his eyes rolling back in his head, his spine connected to a flickering blue server-pillar. 

"The Loom didn't just 'Filter' the trauma; it 'Integrated' it. And the integration? The integration is a heart that must be paid in blood. If you don't find the 'Asset-X' file, the city is going to become a single, collective migraine that will never end." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Hana was back in the ruins. Jiro was still coughing, his eyes rolled back in his head. But the copper trees were still humming. 

"The Loom wasn't a cage, Ren!" Hana screamed into her comm-link. 

"Ren, look at the Arisawa-Cache! It's a 'Filter-Log'!" 

Ren Sato appeared from the shadow of a rusted mag-lev track, his charcoal suit turned a deep, matte-black. His hand was on a heavy, tactile-interface that smelled of dust and unedited logic. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It says I’m the one who saves the city." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the deadzone flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'GHOST-MESSAGE' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

"Welcome to the 'DATA-STORM' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final laugh smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited logic. 

Hana didn't yielding. 

She grabbed Ren’s hand. 

"The Ghost of Arisawa is over, Ren," she said. 

"Welcome to the 'DATA-STORM' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Pursuit had reached its limit. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
