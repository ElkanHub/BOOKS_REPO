# Chapter 29: The Audit of the Soul

The "Audit of the Soul" wasn't a deletion or a data-spike. It was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the Architect into a single, collective laboratory. It sat within the "Sub-Basements" of the Kanda Tower, hidden in the clinical white noise of the tower’s primary server-grid. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the audit-chamber, his charcoal suit turned a deep, matte-black. Above him, the rain was a relentless grey curtain, but it no longer tasted of ozone. The high-frequency hum that had defined his world for a decade was gone. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of data. 

In the center of the chamber stood the "Collective-Memory-Core"—the obsidian sphere that currently held the city’s unedited history. And in the center of the core was the file labeled: *SATO, REN (ARCHITECT TEMPLATE #1).* 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," a voice said, sounding like his own voice from the memory. 

But it wasn't the Director’s voice—dry, cynical, and devoid of any human warmth. It was a recorded pulse, a "Primal-Code" left for the Architect in case the Tower’s AI ever turned against its masters. 

Ren didn't type. He "Synced." 

His hand brushed against the reader, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the mud. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'AUDIT-LEVEL-1' ENGAGED.* 
*TARGET: ASSET_MEMORY_DIRECTOR_SATO_LOGS.* 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

He looked at the screen. It showed the unedited record of his life as Director Sato—the harvestings, the "Self-Death" experiments, the "Quiet-Death" orphanage.

"You don't want to live with the truth, Ren. You've seen the cost. You've seen the 'Election of Echoes.' Why not just 'Audit' the Director into the dark and be the Savior the donors want you to be?" 

Ren didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at Hana. She appeared from the shadow of a server-pillar, her lopsided smile matching the photo. 

"No," Ren whispered, his voice sounding suddenly like a prayer. 

"The promotion is over, Tanaka." 

He slammed the Sync-Lever into the reader. 

*RETENTION PROTOCOL 'PERSONAL-HISTORY' ENGAGED.* 

"Wait," Hana whispered, her eyes filling with a horrific, unedited emotion. 

"You're... you're keeping it?" 

"management," Ren smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

"If I delete the Director, Hana, I’m just making him into a 'Recorded Future' all over again. I’m choosing to remember the cost of the harvest so the city doesn't have to. I’m choosing to be the 'History-Manager' who doesn't take a commission." 

Suddenly, a massive, rhythmic vibration hit the tower. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective conscience. 

The server-pillars throughout the city flickered and died. The tower’s primary architecture geysered sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

"Ren! Ren, it's working!" Hana cried, her hand geysering sparks and blue electricity from her Siphon.

Ren didn't yielding. 

He grabbed Hana’s hand. 

"The Siege of Kanda is over, Hana," he said. 

"Welcome to the 'EPILOGUE' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Resolve had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
