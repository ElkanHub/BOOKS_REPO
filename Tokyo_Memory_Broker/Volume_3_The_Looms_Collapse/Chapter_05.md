# Chapter 05: The Nihilist's Greeting

The "Nihilist's Greeting" wasn't a handshake or a data-spike. It was a "Null-Pulse"—a high-frequency burst of white-noise that turned the Market of Whispers into a single, collective migraine of unedited logic. 

Hana stood in the center of the market square, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen donors were lying on the floor, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to a flickering blue server-pillar that had just geysered into the dark. 

The bombing didn't use geysers or fire. It used "True-Deletion" data-packets. 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the square. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective conscience. 

Hana looked at the billboard in the center of the square. It didn't show Director Sato anymore. It showed a single, clinical-white screen. 

*NO PAST. NO PAIN,* the message appeared on the screen, its letters geysering sparks and high-frequency white-noise. 

"The promotion is complete, Hana," a voice smoothed into her brain—not Ren’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Nihilists. 

"I don't need a fix," a man named Jiro roared, his voice sounding through the static. 

He was standing on top of a tattered billing-column, his matte-black flight jacket zipped tight against the cold. His skin was pale, mapped with the silver scars of a dozen extractions. But his eyes weren't clear of the white-noise cataracts. They were black, light-swallowing abysses. 

"The Grey Collective is a lie, Child!" Jiro cried, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"You're the one who told us that a single memory of a mother's smile was worth more than a dozen 'Blank-Slate' futures. But the 'Debt-Wipe' doesn't just clear the books; it clears the ground! We don't want a history! We don't want a soul! We want the 'Year-Zero'!" 

He raised his resonance stave. 

In his hand was a "Null-Shard"—a device designed to trigger a "Level-10 Deletion" in anything it touched. 

"Yield, Child!" Jiro laughed, a wet, rattling sound that made Hana think of a dying radio. 

"The 'Recorded Future' says you’re the one who deletes the city." 

Hana fell to her knees. Her eyes turned a dark, bruised violet. She wasn't a hero anymore. She was a donor. 

The image of her mother’s smile flickered in her brain—the woman with the sharp, intelligent face and the lopsided smile. 

"The memory is a lie!" Jiro screamed, his hand going to his own spinal port. 

Suddenly, the red lights in the square flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'NIHILIST-STRIKE' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK DISCONNECTED.* 

Hana didn't yield. 

Instead, she grabbed her Siphon and slammed it into the ground. 

"Ren!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

"Ren, it's not the Loom! It's the Nihilists! They're triggers!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Nihilist's Greeting was over. The Siege of Kanda was beginning.
