# Chapter 16: The Great Wipe

The "Great Wipe" wasn't a fire or a data-spike. It was a "Blank-Slate" silence—a high-frequency burst of white-noise that turned the city of Neo-Tokyo into a single, collective migraine of lost logic. 

Ren Sato sat on the floor of the ruins of the Kanda Tower, his charcoal suit shredded, his skin pale and cold. Above him, the rain was a relentless grey curtain, but it no longer tasted of ozone. The high-frequency hum that had defined his world for a decade was gone. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of rubble. 

Beside him, Hana was staring at her own hands. They weren't steady. They were flickering with a pale, ghostly light. 

"Ren..." she whispered, her voice a dry rasp. 

"I don't remember how to zip my jacket." 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at their own hands. All hearing the same promise. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final voice smoothed into his brain—not a recording, but a live, direct neural-link. 

"You've seen the cost. You've seen the 'Election of Echoes.' And now? Now the city is finally free of its own history. Every soul in Neo-Tokyo is currently a 'Blank-Draft'." 

Ren looked out at the city. It wasn't a "Market of Whispers" anymore. It was a "Void." 

People were sitting in the mud of Shinjuku and Shibuya, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to flickering blue server-pillars that were currently geysering sparks and white-noise. 

A man in a tattered business suit was staring at a microwave as if it were a bomb. A woman was staring at her own child, her lopsided smile matching the photo, but her eyes devoid of any human recognition. 

"They've forgotten everything, Ren," Hana cried, her hand geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. 

"They don't remember how to drive. They don't remember how to cook. They don't even remember how to *love*." 

She grabbed his hand. 

"Wait," Ren whispered.
