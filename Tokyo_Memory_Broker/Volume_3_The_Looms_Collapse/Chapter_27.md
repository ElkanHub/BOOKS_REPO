# Chapter 27: The Sync-Election

The "Sync-Election" wasn't a broadcast; it was a "Collective-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma and hope that turned the city of Neo-Tokyo into a single, collective polling-station. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the ruins of the Kanda Tower’s Service-Plinth, his hand on a heavy, tactile-interface that smelled of dust and unedited logic. Beside him, the dozen neighborhood leaders were currently voting. 

They weren't "Syncing" with the Loom. They were "Drafting" a future. 

"The promotion is complete, Neo-Tokyo," Ren whispered, his voice sounding through the static. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at the final ballot on the CRT monitor—not a clinical-white reader, but a tattered piece of Syndicate hardware that had just shown him his own cost. 

*ISSUE #30: THE TOTAL INTEGRATION OF COLLECTIVE HISTORY.* 
*OPTIONS:* 
*1. YEAR-ZERO (Total Deletion)* 
*2. THE-GREY-COLLECTIVE (Full Retention)*.
