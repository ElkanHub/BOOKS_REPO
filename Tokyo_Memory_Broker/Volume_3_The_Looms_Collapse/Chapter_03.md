# Chapter 03: The Grey Council

The "Grey Council" wasn't a boardroom; it was a "Collective-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective consciousness. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the ruins of the Kanda Tower’s Service-Plinth, his matte-black suit humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. Opposite him stood a dozen neighborhood leaders—the "Asset-Archivists" who had survived the Loom’s deactivation and the Syndicate’s purge. 

They weren't wearing lab-coats or charcoal silk. They were wearing rags, their skin pale and mapped with the silver scars of a thousand low-level extractions. But their eyes were clear of the white-noise cataracts, and for the first time in Volume 3, they were staring at him with a professional, detached logic. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," a woman said, her voice sounding through the static. 

"We don't need a Savior. We don't need a Director. We need a 'History-Manager'." 

Ren didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of rubble.
