# Chapter 17: The Nihilist Dawn

The "Nihilist Dawn" wasn't a sunrise; it was a "Black-Out"—a high-frequency burst of white-noise that turned the Kanda Tower into a single, light-swallowing monolith of unedited logic. 

Hana stood at the base of the tower’s service-plinth, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, a dozen people were lying on the floor, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to a flickering blue server-pillar that had just been seized by the Nihilists. 

They weren't "Truth-Wave" technicians anymore. They were "Sync-Slaves" to a new master. 

Suddenly, a door in the tower’s maintenance hatch hissed open. 

It wasn't Tanaka. It wasn't an Enforcer. It was Miki. 

Hana’s old friend stood at the edge of the ledge, her matte-black Nihilist suit zipped tight against the cold. In her hand was a resonance stave, its magnetic spike geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain. 

"The promotion is complete, Child," Miki roared—not a broadcast, but a live, direct neural-link that hit Hana’s brain like a physical blow. 

"Welcome to the 'Nihilist Dawn'. Welcome to the 'Year-Zero'!" 

Miki laughed, a wet, rattling sound. 

"The Grey Collective was a lie, Hana-chan! You're the one who told us that a single memory of a mother's smile was worth more than a dozen 'Blank-Slate' futures. But the 'Debt-Wipe' didn't just clear the books; it cleared the ground! We don't want a history! We want the 'Void'!" 

She raised her resonance stave. 

Behind her, a hundred Nihilist soldiers appeared from the shadow of the server racks. They weren't wearing the matte-black suits of the Easers. They were wearing rags, their skin pale and mapped with the silver scars of a thousand extractions. But their eyes weren't clear of the white-noise cataracts. They were black, light-swallowing abysses. 

"No Past. No Pain!" the soldiers roared, their voices sounding like a collective migraine. 

"Yield, Girl! The 'Recorded Future' says you’re the one who deletes the city." 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at the same monolith. All hearing the same promise. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'YEAR-ZERO' ENGAGED.* 
*TARGET: TOTAL_SENSORY_AUDIT.* 
*STATUS: CORE-DELETION INITIATED.* 

"Miki, stop! It's me! It's Hana!" she screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

The girl didn't answer. She didn't even look at her. She "Synced." 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the tower. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of Loom-data designed to scramble unlicensed hardware and overload neural ports. 

The server-pillars flickered and died. The tower’s emergency lights turned an angry red. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'NIHILIST-CONTROL' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

"Welcome to the 'STATIC WASTES' phase,佐藤さん," Tanaka’s final laugh smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and ancient logic. 

Hana didn't yield. 

She grabbed her weapon. 

"Ren!" she screamed, her voice a dry rasp. 

"We have to go save the city! The Year-Zero is happening!" 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Nihilist Dawn was over. The Siege had begun.
