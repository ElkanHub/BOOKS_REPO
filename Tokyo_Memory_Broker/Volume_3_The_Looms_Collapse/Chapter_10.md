# Chapter 10: The Data-Storm

The "Data-Storm" didn't bring thunder or lightning. It brought "Static-Hail"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited data that turned the rain into a geyser of white-noise and ancient ozone. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the Electrical Deadzone in Akihabara, his matte-black suit humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. Opposite him stood the "Asset-Archivists" who had survived the Loom’s deactivation and the Syndicate’s purge. 

Around them, the copper trees were geysering sparks and blue electricity into the dark. But the sparks didn't just flicker. They "Synced." 

Suddenly, a massive, rhythmic vibration hit the ground. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective conscience. 

Ren looked at his hands. They weren't steady. They were flickering with a pale, ghostly light that made him think of a dying radio. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," a voice smoothed into his brain—not Tanaka’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Loom. 

"The storm isn't a cloud, Ren," Hana cried from the shadow of a server rack. 

She appeared from the shadows, her flight jacket zipped tight against the cold. But her eyes weren't clear of the white-noise cataracts. They were full of a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"It's a 'Filter-Failure'. Without the Master-Loom to curate the donors’ joy, the city's atmosphere is trying to 'Self-Repair' by making every drop of rain experience the same pain. It’s trying to build a 'Perfect Future' out of the rubble of our mistakes." 

Suddenly, a piece of hail hit the ground. It didn't melt. It "Deleted." 

The concrete where it hit turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

"The storm is a 'True-Deletion' engine, Ren!" Hana screamed, her hand geysering sparks and blue electricity from her Siphon. 

"If we don't lead the Council to build a 'Neutralizer-Grid', the city is going to become a 'Blank-Slate' forever!" 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes the city." 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at the sky. He looked at the man—the synth-bio ghost—and for the first time in Volume 3, he felt a genuine, unedited emotion. 

He felt *horror*. 

"The Data-Storm is a lie,佐藤さん," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"Because today is the day the future begins." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and ancient logic. 

Ren didn't yielding. 

He grabbed Hana’s hand. 

"The Data-Storm is over, Hana," he said. 

"Welcome to the 'NEGOTIATOR' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Pursuit had reached its limit. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
