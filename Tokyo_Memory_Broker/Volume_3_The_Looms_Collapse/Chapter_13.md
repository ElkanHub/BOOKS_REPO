# Chapter 13: The First Deletion

The "First Deletion" wasn't a fire or a data-spike. It was a "Null-Draft"—a high-frequency burst of white-noise that turned the grey council into a single, collective migraine. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the ruins of the Kanda Tower’s Service-Plinth, his hand on a heavy, tactile-interface that smelled of dust and unedited logic. Beside him, the dozen neighborhood leaders were currently voting on Issue #12: The Collective Archive’s Foundation Record. 

They weren't "Syncing" with the Loom. They were "Drafting" a future. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," a voice said, sounding like his own voice from the memory. 

But it wasn't the Director’s voice—dry, cynical, and devoid of any human warmth. It was a recorded pulse, a "Primal-Code" left for the Architect in case the Tower’s AI ever turned against its masters. 

Suddenly, a loud, rhythmic vibration hit the plinth. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective conscience. 

Ren looked at the console. The voting-stats weren't flickering. They were vanishing. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'NULL-PULSE' ENGAGED.* 
*TARGET: FOUNDATION_ARCHIVE_v1.0* 

Ren's hand slammed into the reader, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the mud. 

"Stop it!" Ren cried, his voice a dry rasp. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

He looked at the screen. The image of the city’s founding—the first "Symmetry-Grid" being built in Shinjuku, the first donors signing the contract—turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The image geysered sparks and blue electricity, its "Sync-Lock" failing under the weight of the Null-Pulse. 

"The Nihilists..." Ren whispered, his eyes fixing on the screen. 

"The promotion is over,佐藤さん," a voice smoothed into his brain—not Tanaka’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Nihilists. 

"We don't want a history! We don't want a soul! We want the 'Year-Zero'!" 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes you," he smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

He slammed the Sync-Lever into the reader. *SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHITECT-OVERRIDE' ENGAGED.* 

Suddenly, the red lights in the tower flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'FIRST-DELETION' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK DISCONNECTED.* 

The foundation archive was gone. Not "Integrated" or "Managed." Wiped. 

The city’s origin was a clinical-white screen. 

Ren felt the weight of the city’s gaze. He could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at him—at the man who had just allowed their own history to be deleted. 

"The First Deletion is over,佐藤さん," Tanaka’s final laugh smoothed into the rain. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCHITECT'S SHADOW' phase." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited logic. 

Ren didn't yielding. 

He grabbed Hana’s hand. 

"The Nihilists are the ones who wrote the ending, Hana," he said. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCHITECT'S SHADOW' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Siege of Kanda was over. The Resolve had begun.
