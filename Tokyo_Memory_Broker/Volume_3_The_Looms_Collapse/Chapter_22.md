# Chapter 22: The Dark Point

The "Dark Point" wasn't a shadow or a blackout; it was a "Null-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of white-noise that turned the Kanda Tower into a single, light-swallowing monolith of lost logic. 

Hana stood in the center of the ruins of the Core-Hangar, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. Around her, the "Collective-Memory-Core" was geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain, its surface mapping with the silver scars of a thousand low-level extractions. 

But the image on the CRT monitor wasn't a "Backup-History" anymore. It was a "Void." 

"Ren! Ren, it's failing!" she screamed into her comm-link. 

But Ren didn't answer. He was standing in front of the master-control lever, his hand geysering sparks and blue electricity into the reader. 

"The promotion is complete, Child," a voice smoothed into Hana’s brain—not Ren’s voice, but a collective, synthesized voice that sounded like a thousand voices combined. 

The Nihilists. 

"No Past. No Pain," the voice whispered, sounding through the static. 

Suddenly, a massive, rhythmic vibration hit the hangar. It wasn't a mechanical vibration; it was a "Pulse-Wave"—a high-frequency burst of Loom-data designed to scramble unlicensed hardware and overload neural ports. 

Hana feel the weight of the city’s gaze. She could feel the thousands of eyes in the Grey Zone, all staring at their own hands. All waiting for a "Sync-Prompt" that would never come. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'NULL-PULSE' ENGAGED.* 
*TARGET: TOTAL_CORE_DELETION.* 
*STATUS: YEAR-ZERO COMMENCED.* 

"Miki, stop!" Hana cried, her hand geysering sparks and blue electricity from her Siphon. 

Miki appeared from the shadow of the server racks, her matte-black Nihilist suit zipped tight against the cold. In her hand was a resonance stave, its magnetic spike geysering sparks and blue electricity into the rain. 

"The promotion is over, Hana-chan!" Miki roared—not a broadcast, but a live, direct neural-link that hit Hana’s brain like a physical blow. 

"Yield, Girl! The 'Recorded Future' says you’re the one who deletes the city." 

Hana fell to her knees. Her eyes turned a dark, bruised violet. She wasn't a hero. She was a donor. 

The image of her mother’s smile flickered in her brain—the woman with the sharp, intelligent face and the lopsided smile. 

"The memory is a lie!" Miki shouted, her hand going to her own spinal port. 

"Ren! Ren, do it now!" Hana screamed, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. 

But the world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The server-pillars flickered and died. The tower’s emergency lights turned an angry red. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'NIHILIST-CONTROL' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK DISCONNECTED.* 

Hana didn't see the flash. She didn't hear the thunder. 

She just felt the "Null." 

Her breath caught in her throat. Her heart stopped. 

"Ren..." she whispered. 

But there was no one there. The world was a blinding, crystalline static. 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final laugh smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and ancient logic. 

The Dark Point was over. The Siege of Kanda was beginning.
