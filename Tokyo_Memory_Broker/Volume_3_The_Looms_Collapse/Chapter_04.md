# Chapter 04: The Viral Thread

The "Viral Thread" wasn't a piece of software; it was a "Neuro-Sync"—a high-frequency burst of raw, unedited trauma that turned the city into a single, collective consciousness. 

Ren Sato sat in the center of the ruins of the Kanda Tower’s Service-Plinth, his hand on a heavy, tactile-interface that smelled of dust and unedited logic. Above him, the rain was a relentless grey curtain, but it no longer tasted of ozone. The high-frequency hum that had defined his world for a decade was gone. 

He didn't have a pulse. He didn't have a breath. He was the "Master-Loom," but he was a god of data. 

He reached for the "Echo-Cough" code-packet—a small, flickering Shard that had been scavenged from Jiro’s spinal port. 

"The promotion is complete, Sato-san," a voice said, sounding like his own voice from the memory. 

But it wasn't the Director’s voice—dry, cynical, and devoid of any human warmth. It was a recorded pulse, a "Primal-Code" left for the Architect in case the Tower’s AI ever turned against its masters. 

Ren didn't type. He "Synced." 

His hand brushed against the reader, his spinal port geysering sparks and blue electricity into the mud. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ECHO-COUGH' ANALYZED.* 
*STATUS: SELF-REPAIR PROTOCOL 'SYMMETRY' v1.12* 
*FUNCTION: COLLECTIVE SENSORY-ALIGNMENT.* 

Ren Sato stood up. His synth-bio body felt suddenly tight, the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull humming with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

He looked at the terminal screen. The code wasn't a virus. It wasn't a deletion. 

It was the Loom. 

"The Tower... it's trying to come home, Hana," Ren whispered, his voice sounding through the static. 

"The Echo-Cough isn't a disease; it's a 'Filter-Failure'. Without the Master-Loom to curate the donors’ joy, the city is trying to 'Self-Repair' by making everyone experience everyone else’s trauma at once. It’s trying to build a 'Perfect Future' out of the rubble of our mistakes." 

He pointed toward the code-packet. 

"Look at the 'Sync-Lock.' Tanaka didn't just delete the records; he 'De-Fragmented' them. Every soul in Neo-Tokyo is currently trying to 'Symmetry' with every other soul, turning the city into a single, collective mind of unedited logic." 

Hana appeared from the shadow of a server-pillar, her Siphon pulsing a dim, exhausted blue. But her eyes weren't full of disgust anymore. They were full of a rhythmic, violent energy. 

"The Nihilists... they're using the Viral Thread, Ren," she hissed, her eyes fixing on the screen. 

"They're trying to trigger a 'Year-Zero' by making the Loom’s 'Self-Repair' protocol permanent. If we don't lead the Council to build a 'Neutralizer-Grid', the city is going to become a 'Blank-Slate' forever." 

Ren looked at the screen. He looked at the man—the synth-bio ghost—and for the first time in Volume 3, he felt a genuine, unedited emotion. 

He felt *fear*. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his hand going to his head. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes the city." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the tower flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'VIRAL-THREAD' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

"Welcome to the 'ARCIVE'S LIE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed into the silence. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and ancient ozone. 

Ren didn't yielding. 

He grabbed Hana’s hand. 

"The Viral Thread is over, Hana," he said. 

"Welcome to the 'NIHILIST'S GREETING' phase." 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The Pursuit had reached its limit. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
