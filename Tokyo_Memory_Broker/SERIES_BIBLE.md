# SERIES_BIBLE: Tokyo Memory Broker
## Master Reference for the Kiroku Series

**Version:** 1.0
**Project:** Tokyo Memory Broker
**Setting:** Neo-Tokyo, 2084

## PART ONE: SERIES FOUNDATION

### 1.1 — Series Identity
**Series Title:** Tokyo Memory Broker  
**Series Tagline:** In a world that forgets, the only thing more dangerous than a secret is a truth you've already lived.

**Volume 1 Title:** The First Shard  
**Volume 2 Title:** Echoes of the Syndicate  
**Volume 3 Title:** The Loom's Collapse  
**Volume 4 Title:** Blood and Data  
**Volume [FINAL] Title:** The Last Remembrance

### 1.2 — Internal Series Summary
Ren Sato is a professional "memory broker" in the rain-slicked streets of Neo-Tokyo. In a future where the wealthy "play" the traumatic memories of the poor for entertainment, Ren is a top harvester. His life is built on the extracted pain of others, but his own past is a blur—a "clean slate" he attributes to his professional training. While harvesting a rare "Self-Death" fragment from a dying Syndicate scientist, Ren sees himself in the memory—dying years ago. He discovers he is a "Rebirth," a human vessel wiped and refilled with composite data. The series follows his journey to dismantle the Kanda Syndicate and reclaim his original self, even if that self was a monster.

### 1.3 — Genre Stack
**Primary Genre:** Neo-noir Psychological Thriller  
**Genre Layer 2:** Cyberpunk  
**Genre Layer 3:** Supernatural (Spirit-tech)  
**Tonal References:** *Blade Runner 2049*, *Psycho-Pass*, *Perfect Blue*.

### 1.4 — Central Dramatic Question
Can a person built from stolen truths ever reclaim their own identity?

### 1.5 — Series Transformation Arc
- **Beginning:** Ren is a cynical commodity trader of human misery, detached and efficient.
- **End:** Ren accepts the full weight of his original trauma and the pain he caused, choosing to become a guardian of memory rather than its parasite.
- **Permanent Change:** The "Loom" (memory infrastructure) is destroyed, forcing humanity to face their own pain again.

## PART TWO: THEMATIC ARCHITECTURE

### 2.1 — Core Theme
**The commodification of human experience.**  
The world treats memories not as sacred history but as liquid assets to be traded, diluted, or deleted for profit.

### 2.2 — Supporting Themes
1. **Identity as a Construct:** If your memories are fake, who are you?
2. **The Ethics of Forgetting:** Is peace worth the price of the truth?
3. **The Cost of Empathy:** Consuming pain makes you understand others, but at what cost to your own sanity?

### 2.3 — The Thematic Argument
Suppressing trauma for profit or comfort doesn't erase it; it only creates a hunger that eventually consumes the individual and society.

## PART THREE: WORLD-BUILDING

### 3.1 — Core World Concept
Neo-Tokyo 2084: A world where human emotions and memories are physically extracted via spinal "Loom-needles," distilled into "Kiroku" (Memory Shards), and sold to the elite.

### 3.2 — The World's Defining Rules
1. **Permanence:** Once a memory is extracted, the donor forgets it forever.
2. **Echo-rot:** Consuming too many Shards causes "Echo-rot"—ghost-memories that overwrite the consumer's base personality.
3. **Purity State:** Traumatic memories are the most "potent" and valuable because they trigger the strongest dopamine/adrenal response.
4. **The Loom:** The satellite-linked network that stabilizes Shard-integration. Controlled by the Kanda Syndicate.

### 3.3 — Power Systems
**Kiroku (Shard-tech):**
- **Capabilities:** Emotional transfer, skill-download (unstable), sensory playback.
- **Price:** Deep-seated psychological instability, addiction, and eventually the loss of the "Core Self."

### 3.4 — Factions
- **Kanda Syndicate:** Corporate-led mafia that owns the Loom. They want total control of the city's collective memory.
- **The Hollowed:** An underground resistance of donors who have lost too much and want to "Remember the Silence."
- **Brokers:** Independent harvesters like Ren who operate in the gray zones of Akihabara and Shinjuku.

## PART FOUR: CHARACTER ARCHITECTURE

### 4.1 — Character Profiles

#### CHARACTER PROFILE: REN SATO
- **Surface:** Sharp, tailored suits, cold eyes, expert in de-escalating donor-panic.
- **The Wound:** A "hole" where his childhood should be. He was told it was the price of his Broker license.
- **The Lie:** "The past doesn't matter, only the present value of a Shard."
- **The Truth:** He was the original architect of the Loom who tried to shut it down and was "reset" as punishment.
- **Voice Sample:** "Your pain has a market value, Hana. Don't waste it on tears. Save them for the extraction."

#### CHARACTER PROFILE: DIRECTOR DAISUKE TANAKA
- **Surface:** Elder statesman of the Syndicate, obsessed with "order."
- **The Argument:** "Humanity is a chaotic mess of trauma. We provide the pruning shears."
- **Relationship:** Ren's "mentor" and the man who ordered his reset.

#### CHARACTER PROFILE: HANA
- **Surface:** A "hollowed" girl living in the ruins of Shibuya.
- **Role:** Mirror to Ren. She gave her memories to pay for her brother's life-support.
- **Need:** To find a reason to exist without her past.

## PART FIVE: PLOT ARCHITECTURE

### 5.1 — Series Spine
- **Vol 1: The First Shard** — Ren finds the fragment of his own death.
- **Vol 2: Echoes of the Syndicate** — Ren infiltrates the Kanda HQ and finds his "file."
- **Vol 3: The Loom's Collapse** — The city begins to suffer from "Collective Echo-rot."
- **Vol 4: Blood and Data** — Ren leads the Hollowed in a literal war for the Loom.
