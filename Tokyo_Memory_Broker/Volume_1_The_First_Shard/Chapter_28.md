# Chapter 28: The Ending Revelation

The silence of Neo-Tokyo was louder than any static. 

Ren Sato lay in the rubble of the Kanda Tower, his charcoal suit shredded, his skin pale and cold. Above him, the rain was a relentless grey curtain, but it no longer tasted of ozone. The high-frequency hum that had defined his world for a decade was gone. 

The city was learning how to remember its own pain again. 

Ren tried to move. His body was a collection of heavy, unresponsive weights. His fingers were numb, and the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull was a dark, brooding violet. 

He reached for his chest, his hand searching for the rhythmic, comforting beat of a human heart. 

He didn't find one. 

His chest didn't move. There was no pulse. No breath. 

Ren Sato wasn't breathing. 

He reached for the "Med-Kit" in his pocket—not the makeshift med-kit of Arisawa, but a high-end Syndicate injector. He slammed it into his thigh. 

The injector hissed, but it didn't geyser sparks or blue electricity. It didn't do anything. 

"Yield, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed from the rubble. 

"The promotion is complete. Welcome to the 'NULL' phase." 

Ren looked at his own hands. They were steady. Impeccably manicured. But they were the hands of a statue. 

He reached for a piece of shattered lead-glass and studied his reflection. 

His eyes were clear, the diamond-like brilliance gone. But his skin didn't have the silver scars of a donor. It didn't have the rhythmic, red light of a Broker. 

It was perfect. It was clinical. 

"A 'Live-Data' vessel," Ren whispered, his voice a dry rasp that didn't sound like his own. 

He wasn't a man who had been reset. He was a body that had been "Drafted." 

He was a high-grade synth-bio vessel, designed to hold the "ARCHITECT" template without the limitation of a human lifespan. He didn't have a heartbeat because he didn't have a heart. He had a data-core. 

"Ren! Ren, come on!" 

Hana's voice—not the Executive, but the Ghost-Hana—echoed from the rubble. She was leading a dozen Hollowed through the wreckage, their matte-black suits reflecting the flickering blue light of the Pulse-Wave. 

She stopped beside him, her hands shaking as she pulled a Siphon from her bag. 

"Sato? Sato, look at me!" 

Ren looked at her. He looked at the girl—at the ghost—and for the first time in his life, he felt a genuine, unedited emotion. 

He felt *grief*. 

"I'm not here, Hana," Ren whispered, his hand reaching for the Diamond Shard. 

"What do you mean?" Hana asked, her voice sounding suddenly sharp. 

"I'm a 'Recorded Future'," Ren said, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"I'm the first one, Hana. I'm the one who can never die because I was never really alive. I'm the one who built the cage, and I’m the only one who can’t leave it." 

He handed the Shard to her. 

"The tower is active," Ren whispered, his hand going to the siler-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"Welcome to the 'NULL' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the server-pillar’s speakers. 

Suddenly, the red lights in the ruins flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'HIVE-MIND' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

Ren didn't yield. He "Synced." 

He gave his own data-core to the city. He gave his "Self-Death" memory to the Master-Loom. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Ren Sato closed his eyes, and for the first time in ten years, he didn't remember the cost. 

He was the silence. And the silence was the only... future. 
The First Shard was broken. But the Loom was still hungry.
"Welcome to the 'LAST REMEMBRANCE' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed from the data-dust. 

"Try not to get deleted."
