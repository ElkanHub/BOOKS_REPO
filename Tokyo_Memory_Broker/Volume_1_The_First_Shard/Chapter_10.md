# Chapter 10: The Market for Misery

The "Lounge of Eternal Echoes" sat atop a ninety-story spire in Shinjuku, a temple of gold-glass and silent, pressurized air. Here, the Syndicate’s most elite clients spent their evenings consuming the refined, unedited trauma of the poor, seeking the one thing their money couldn't buy: a soul with weight. 

Ren Sato adjusted the collar of his "Borrow-Suit"—a high-end charcoal silk that he’d stolen from a Broker’s safehouse in the Grey Zones. He looked like exactly who he was supposed to be: a successful trader of human misery, returning from a high-value harvest. 

Beside him, Hana was dressed as his "Silent-Aide," her tattered flight jacket replaced by a sleek, form-fitting biosuit that hid her scars and her Siphon. Her eyes were lowered, her expression a mask of professional submission. 

"Stay calm," Ren whispered as they approached the velvet-draped entrance. "The biometric scanners are tuned to my old signature. If we don't trigger the 'Broker-Blacklist,' we have ten minutes before Tanaka’s Pulse-Wave clears the district." 

Hana didn't answer. She didn't have to. She was already busy scanning the Lounge’s wireless network, her neural port humming with a rhythmic, red light. 

"Identity," the lounge’s AI smoothed, its voice a synthesized velvet. 

Ren held up his license—the physical one, etched in silver and stamped with the Kanda seal. The scanner’s red laser traced the contours of the Syndicate eagle. 

"Broker 4-0-9. Sato, Ren. Welcome back, Sato-san. Director Tanaka is expecting you at the Gala." 

"I'm not here for the Gala," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. "I have a high-priority harvest that needs 'Deep-Calibration' before it’s released to the market." 

"Of course. Proceed to Calibration Suite 4. The Deep-Reader is primed." 

They walked through the lounge, the air thick with the scent of high-grade coolant and expensive synthetic cherry blossoms. Around them, clients sat in plush, velvet chairs, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to individual "Loom-Hooks" as they consumed the Shards of the day. 

They looked like ghosts. They looked like gods. 

Calibration Suite 4 was a private room, shielded against the ambient noise of the lounge. In the center sat the Deep-Reader—a sleek, obsidian sphere surrounded by a dozen delicate, bioluminescent needles. 

Ren pulled the Diamond Shard from his sleeve. 

"Don't let them read the 'Beacon' signature," Hana said, moving to the Deep-Reader’s console. "If they get a hit on the Arisawa's code, the whole building goes into lockdown." 

She began to type, her hands moving with a fluid, mechanical grace. She was bypassing the Syndicate’s security filters, creating a "Parallel-Loop" that would feed the Diamond Shard’s data into a local buffer without alerting the Master-Loom. 

Ren placed the Diamond Shard on the Deep-Reader’s pedestal. 

The obsidian sphere pulsed once, a final, violent surge of white light, and then turned a deep, brooding violet. 

"What is this?" Ren hissed, staring at the playback on the wall-mounted screen. 

It wasn't a memory of a life. It wasn't a memory of a death. 

It was a "Signed" file. 

The image on the screen wasn't a visual from a person's life. It was a digital signature—a complex, multi-layered code that carried the watermark of the Kanda Syndicate's Most-Secretive Architect. 

"The Diamond Shard..." Hana whispered, her eyes fixed on the code. "It’s not just an Override, Ren. It’s the 'Parent-Key.'" 

She pointed to the signature at the bottom of the screen. 

*AUTHORIZED BY: DIRECTOR SATO, R.*

"It’s signed by you," Hana said, her voice sounding suddenly sharp. "But... it was never meant to be harvested. It was never meant to exist." 

She pointed to the "Creation-Date" on the file. 

*SEPTEMBER 12, 2084.*

"That's tomorrow," Ren said, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"Yes," Hana said, her eyes widening. "Because this isn't a memory of the past, Ren. It's a 'Recorded-Future.' The Loom isn't just extracting history anymore. It’s writing it." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the suite flashed once. A warning. 

*LOCKDOWN IN PROGRESS. SECURITY PROTOCOL 'ARCHITECT-ERROR' ENGAGED.* 

"They found the Parent-Key," Hana hissed, grabbing her bag and the Siphon. "The Master-Loom just realized its own creator is trying to delete it." 

Ren grabbed the Diamond Shard and the photograph. 

"We have to go," Ren said, his hand going to his Spike-Knife. 

The suite’s door hissed shut, and the sounds of a dozen Easers hitting the velvet-carpeted lounge filled the air. 

"Welcome to the Market for Misery, Director," Hana said, her hand going to the suite’s emergency release. "Try not to get sold." 

The lockdown hit, and for a heartbeat, the world was nothing but static and white-noise.
