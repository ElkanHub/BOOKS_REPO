# Chapter 4: Escape from Sector 7

The nitrogen fog geysered from the storage tanks, a wall of absolute white that swallowed Tanaka and his Easers. For a heartbeat, the lab was plunged into a cryogenic silence, broken only by the hiss of the escaping gas and the snap of freezing electronics.

Ren didn't wait for the Easers to recalibrate their thermal sensors. 

He didn't run for the door. Tanaka would be expecting that. Instead, Ren dove through the hole the first Ripper drone had made in the residential tower’s wall. He scrambled up a leaning support beam, his boots skidding on the frost-covered metal, his breath coming in jagged, crystalline bursts.

Behind him, the fog was already being dissipated by high-frequency sonar blasts.

"Sato-san!" Tanaka’s voice was calm, amplified by the Easers’ comms. "You’re making a category-one error. The Shard cannot be read by unlicensed hardware. It will rot your brain before you find a reader."

Ren didn't answer. He reached the second floor of the derelict electronics market, a graveyard of hol-emitters and CRT skeletons. He could hear the Ripper drones clicking as they recalibrated, their sonic pulses bouncing off the twisted metal.

He needed a distraction.

Ren reached into his belt and pulled out a "Static-Grenade"—a crudely made device he’d traded for a "Drunken Night" Shard in a Shinjuku back-alley. He primed it and rolled it down the center aisle of the market.

*THWUMP.*

The grenade didn't explode in fire. It exploded in light—a massive, localized burst of electromagnetic noise that mimicked the signature of a high-value harvest.

To the Ripper drones, it was like a flare in the dark. Six of them dove toward the static, their blades spinning into a frenzy as they tried to harvest the ghost-signal.

Ren used the chaos to leap through a shattered window.

He hit the data-dust of the street ten feet below, his knees absorbing the shock. The rain was colder here, mixing with the dust to form a grey slurry that clung to his coat. 

Sector 7 was a labyrinth of narrow alleys and collapsed structures. Ren knew the map by heart—or at least the version of the map the Syndicate didn't bother to update. He ducked into a drainage pipe, the smell of ancient sewage and ozone thick in his throat.

He could hear the Easers now. They weren't calling out names. They were moving in a formation, their resonance staves hummed with a low, rhythmic throb that vibrated in Ren’s molars.

*Thump. Thump. Thump.*

It was the sound of a closing net.

Ren reached the end of the pipe, which opened onto a canal of stagnant, oily water. He stayed low, the diamond Shard in his sleeve feeling like a shard of ice against his skin.

Suddenly, the water erupted.

A Ripper drone, its optical sensor glowing a malevolent violet, surged from the canal. It had been waiting in the "dead" water, its sensors tuned to the specific bio-rhythm of Ren’s synth-bio body.

Ren rolled to the side as the drone's blades shredded the concrete where he’d been crouching. He pulled his own "Spike-Knife"—a weapon designed to disrupt the magnetic field of Loom-tech—and drove it into the drone’s central processor.

The drone spasmed, its blades slowing to a halt. It sunk into the water with a hiss.

Ren didn't stop to admire his work. He scrambled up the canal wall and into a cluster of rusted shipping containers. 

Half an hour later, he was two miles away, hidden in the shadow of a collapsed mag-lev pylon. The sounds of the chase had faded into the ambient static of the deadzone.

Ren slumped against the cold metal, his heart—or the mechanical pump that served as his heart—racing. He reached into his sleeve and pulled out the diamond Shard.

It was silent. It was beautiful. 

But as he stared at the crystalline surface, he noticed something. A faint, red light was pulsing deep within the diamond. It wasn't the white light of Arisawa’s memory. It was a rhythmic, steady blink.

Ren’s blood ran cold. 

The "Self-Death" Shard wasn't just a record. It was a "Beacon-Shard." By harvesting it, he hadn't just taken Arisawa’s secrets. He’d taken a Syndicate tracer that was now broadcasting his exact coordinates to every tower in Neo-Tokyo.

He looked up. 

In the distance, the high-altitude lights of the Syndicate’s "Director-Class" carrier drones were already turning toward his position.

Ren didn't curse. He didn't have the breath for it. He tucked the Shard back into its hidden compartment and looked toward Shibuya.

He needed someone who knew how to "Black-Out" a Shard. He needed the girl in the photograph.
