# Chapter 29: The Last Remembrance

Shibuya was breathing. 

It wasn't the rhythmic, synthetic heartbeat of the Loom anymore. It was the jagged, unpredictable breath of a city that had forgotten how to be itself. The rain no longer tasted of ozone; it tasted of soot and old copper, the familiar, grounding taste of the old world. 

Hana sat on the steps of the collapsed Shibuya 109 building, her flight jacket zipped tight against the cold. Beside her, the "Echo-Pile" in the center of the ruins was gone, replaced by a field of data-dust and shattered glass. 

The donors were no longer staring at their Shards. They were walking through the streets, their eyes clear, their hands reaching out to touch the rain. Some were crying. Some were laughing. All of them were remembering. 

"The tower is still active, Hana," a voice whispered from her comm-link—not the collective voice of the Loom, but the dry, gravelly rasp of the Elder. 

"But it’s not harvesting anymore," Hana said, her voice sounding suddenly sharp. 

"It's 'Circulating'." 

She looked up at the Kanda Tower, a black, jagged monolith against the grey sky. It wasn't pulsing with red or white light. It was glowing with a soft, bioluminescent blue. 

Ren Sato’s bio-signature. 

The Architect had become the "Master-Loom," but he wasn't prescriptive. He was the guardian. He was the one who made sure the memories of the city were shared, not stolen. 

Suddenly, a figure stepped out from the shadow of a collapsed arcade. 

It wasn't an Enforcer. It wasn't an Easer. It was a man in a tailored charcoal suit, his skin mapped with the silver scars of a thousand low-level extractions. 

Kobi. 

He didn't raise his resonance stave. He raised his hand. 

In his hand was a small, brilliant diamond Shard. It wasn't a "Fresh Trauma" Shard. It was something else. 

"Looking for a fix, Girl?" Kobi smiled, his teeth yellowed by Loom-residue. 

Hana tightens her grip on her Siphon. "I don't need a fix, Kobi. The Loom is deactivated." 

"The Loom isn't deactivated, Hana-chan," Kobi laughed, a wet, rattling sound. "It’s 'Re-Calibrated.' The city doesn't need trauma anymore. It needs 'Joy.' It needs the memories of the things it’s already lost." 

He held up the diamond Shard. 

"The first 'Joy-Shard' of the new season," Kobi smiled. "I harvested it from a donor in the Upper Wards. She remembered the sound of her mother’s voice. She remembered the taste of the first peach she ever ate." 

He moved closer, his hand reaching for the Siphon. 

"The Syndicate is gone, Hana. But the 'Market for Misery' is just the beginning. Now... now we have the 'Market for Hope'." 

Hana looked at the shard. She looked at Kobi—at the man who had sold them for a promotion. 

"I'm not a client, Kobi," Hana said, her voice sounding like a promise. 

"I'm the Broker." 

She slammed her Siphon into the diamond Shard, the magnetic spike geysering sparks and high-frequency noise. The "Joy-memory" didn't dissolve. It "Echoed." 

Suddenly, the people around them stopped. They weren't staring at Kobi. They were staring at Hana. 

"The Last Remembrance," Hana whispered, her hand going to the siler-ringed port at the base of her own skull. 

The world turned from blue to a blinding, crystalline white. 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Shibuya was breathing. And the Broker was the only one who didn't remember the cost. 
"Welcome to the 'IMAGE OF THE VOID' phase, Hana," Tanaka’s final whisper smoothed from the rubble. 

"Try not to get deleted." 
Hana stood up, her face a mask of cold, focused determination. She didn't reach for the Diamond Shard. She reached for the Siphon. 

She wasn't Arisawa’s daughter anymore. She was the one who remembered. 
The First Shard was broken. But the Loom was still hungry.
"Welcome to Volume 2," Hana whispered to the silence.
