# Chapter 11: Echo-Rot

Ren Sato was screaming, but there was no sound. 

Hana watched him from the Deep-Reader’s console, her breath coming in shallow, frantic bursts. His body was rigid, his eyes rolled back, his fingers clawing at the velvet upholstery of the suite’s chair. 

The Diamond Shard was still on the pedestal, its blinding white light reflecting off the chrome needles of the reader. It wasn't just data; it was a deluge, a tidal wave of bio-organic code that was rewriting Ren’s internal sensors. 

"Ren!" Hana hissed, her hand going to the reader's emergency release. 

She shouldn't have opened it. The "Calibration-Lockdown" was already in progress, and the Shard’s signature was integrated into the building’s core security. To pull the Shard now was to risk a "Neural-Shatter"—a total deletion of Ren’s base personality. 

But if she didn't stop it, the "Echo-Rot" would finish the job. 

Ren’s skin was pale, his veins glowing a faint, bioluminescent blue. His pulse was a frantic, erratic drum-roll, and his pupil dilation was off the charts. 

"Director... Tanaka..." Ren whispered, his voice sounding suddenly sharp. 

Hana froze. It wasn't Ren’s voice. It wasn't the dry, cynical rasp of the Broker. 

It was the cold, professional voice of Director Sato. 

"The Loom... is hungry," the voice said, Ren’s hand moving with a fluid, mechanical grace as it reached for the Shard. "The history of the world... is just... unwritten... trauma." 

Hana scrambled back, her Siphon pulsing with a malevolent red light. 

"Ren, stop it!" 

The man in the chair didn't stop. He turned his head, his eyes fixing on Hana with a brilliant, diamond-like certainty. 

"Arisawa’s daughter," the voice said, his lips curling into a smile that Ren Sato would never have used. "You're... exquisite. The perfect... commodity." 

He reached out a hand, his fingers touching Hana’s cheek. The touch was cold, sterile, and absolutely devoid of any human warmth. 

"I built the cage for you," he whispered, his eyes widening. "I built the future... one memory at a time. And now... you're going to help me... finish it." 

Suddenly, the red lights in the suite flashed once. A warning. 

*SECURITY PROTOCOL 'HIVE-MIND' ENGAGED. DIRECT NEURAL-LINK ESTABLISHED.* 

"They're reading the Director," Hana hissed, grabbing her bag and the Siphon. "The Master-Loom just found its missing architect." 

She didn't hesitate. She slammed her Siphon into the Deep-Reader’s pedestal, the magnetic spike shattering the obsidian sphere in a shower of sparks and high-frequency noise. 

The white light from the Diamond Shard flickered and died. 

Ren slumped forward, his breath sounding like a death rattle. 

Hana scrambled to him, her hands shaking as she pulled the Loom-needle from his port. Blood was beginning to seep from his ears—a sign of "Feedback Rupture." 

"Ren! Come on, Ren! Wake up!" 

His eyes slowly focused, the diamond-like brilliance replaced by a hollow, flickering confusion. 

"Hana..." Ren said, his voice sounding like himself again. "What... what happened?" 

"Echo-Rot," Hana said, her voice sounding suddenly sharp. "You were speaking in the Director’s voice. You were... you were starting to remember what you did." 

Ren looked at the shattered remains of the Deep-Reader. He looked at Hana, then at his own hands. 

"I felt it," he whispered, his eyes widening. "I felt the Loom. It wasn't a tech-grid. It was a... hunger. A collective hunger for the things we’ve already lost." 

The suite’s door hissed shut, and the sounds of a dozen Easers hitting the velvet-carpeted lounge filled the air. 

"Welcome to the 'Echo-Rot' phase, Sato-san," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the suite’s comms. "Your 'ARCHITECT' template is nearly complete. Give us the Shard, and we can finish the promotion." 

Ren looked at the Diamond Shard in his hand. He looked at Hana, then at the door. 

"I'm not going back to the cage, Tanaka," Ren said, his hand going to his Spike-Knife. 

He didn't hand over the Shard. 

Instead, he grabbed Hana and dove through the suite’s ventilation shaft. 

The Pulse-Wave hit the room, and for a heartbeat, the world was nothing but static and white-noise.
