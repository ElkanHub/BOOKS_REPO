# Chapter 25: The Choice of the Shard

The roof of the Kanda Tower was a cathedral of rain and static. The bioluminescent signs of Shinjuku were dying, replaced by the rhythmic, angry red of a thousand overlapping memories that had been "Drafted" into the city’s collective consciousness. 

Ren Sato stood in the center of the rooftop, his charcoal suit whipping in the wind. In his hand, the Diamond Shard pulsed with a soft, bioluminescent blue—not the cold, clinical white of the Syndicate, but the warm, flickering blue of a person who didn't exist yet. 

Beside him, the Ghost-Hana—the girl in the flight jacket—was breathing in shallow, rhythmic bursts, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, malevolent blue as she scanned the internal resonance of the tower. 

Opposite them stood the Executive—the real Hana. She looked exactly like the woman in the file, her eyes like polished diamonds, her face free of any human empathy. She was holding a Loom-needle, its tip glowing with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

Between them, Director Tanaka sat in a hover-chair, his tailored suit impeccably dry even in the damp air. He looked like exactly who he was: the Hand of the Syndicate. 

"The choice is simple, Sato-san," Tanaka said, his voice smooth and professional. 

"The 'Recorded Future' says you’re the one who deletes the city," he smiled, his hand going to his own spinal port. 

"But the 'Director’s Template' says you’re the one who saves it. Give us the Shard, and we can finishing the promotion. You can have your safehouse back. You can have your status back. You can have your *past* back." 

Ren looked at the Shard in his hand. He looked at the Executive—at the woman who shared his biological history. Then he looked at Ghost-Hana—at the girl who shared his survival. 

"The past is a curated fiction, Tanaka," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

He didn't raise his Spike-Knife. He didn't have to. 

"I remember what the 'Recorded Future' says," Ren whispered, his hand going to his own spinal port. 

"It says I’m the one who deletes you," Ren smiled, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

Suddenly, a high-frequency hum began to resonate from his body. It wasn't the sound of a human heartbeat. It was the "Architect-Pulse"—the signal that told the Master-Loom its creator was back. 

But it wasn't the Director’s pulse. It was the Broker’s pulse. 

Ren wasn't using the data Tanaka had curated for him. He was using the data he had saved for himself—the memories of his favorite meal in Shinjuku, the record of his first commission, the feeling of the rain in Akihabara. 

He was using his "Personal Anchors." 

"No!" Tanaka roared, his hand going to the readers. 

But the pulse was too strong. It was a "Shared-Trauma"—not the trauma of the donors, but the trauma of the man who had deleted them. 

Ren felt the weight of every soul he had ever harvested. He felt the grief of Arisawa’s daughter. He felt the terror of the first "Self-Death" experiments. 

And then, he gave it all to Tanaka. 

The Director’s brain didn't melt. It "Synced." 

Tanaka’s eyes rolled back in his head, his skin turning a dark, bruised violet. He wasn't human anymore. He was a "Sync-Slave"—a biological node in the Loom’s new architecture. 

The hover-chair slumped forward, the Loom-needle hitting the ground with a rhythmic, violent energy. 

Ren stood over him, his heart—or the mechanical pump that served as his heart—racing. 

He looked at the Executive. She was staring at him, her eyes wide with a horrific, unedited emotion. 

"You chose... her," the Executive whispered, her voice sounding like a threat. 

"I didn't choose her, Hana," Ren said, his voice sounding like a promise. "I chose the ghost." 

He reached for Ghost-Hana’s hand. Her form flickered once, her flight jacket turning to a sleek, charcoal silk suit, then back again. 

"The Diamond Shard," she whispered, her eyes fixed on the Shard. 

Ren handed it to her. 

"The 'Parent-Key'," he whispered, his hand going to the silver-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"Welcome to the 'RESOLVE' phase, Sato-san," Ghost-Hana smiled, her lopsided expression matching the photo. 

Suddenly, the red lights in the city flashed once. A warning. 

*MASTER-LOOM SYMMETRY BREACHED. SYSTEM DELETION IN PROGRESS.* 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the season hit Neo-Tokyo, and for a heartbeat, the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Shinjuku was losing its mind. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost. 
"Welcome to the 'SIEGE' phase, Hana," Ren said as the roof began to collapse. 

Try not to get deleted.
