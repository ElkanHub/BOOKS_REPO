# Chapter 16: The Loom’s Heart

The Kanda Satellite-Link Tower sat in the center of Neo-Tokyo like a jagged, black tooth. It was the only building in the city that didn't use neon; its surface was coated in a specialized "Null-Paint" that absorbed all light and radar, making it a ghost-monolith even in the midday gloom. 

Ren Sato sat on a crate in the Hollowed colony, his head buried in his hands. The static of the Echo-Pile was still humming in his brain, a rhythmic, violent reminder of the man he used to be. 

Beside him, Hana was studying a holographic blueprint of the Tower’s security grid. Her expression was a mask of professional focus, but her hands were shaking. 

"The 'Recorded Future' is a lie, Ren," Hana said, her voice sounding suddenly sharp. 

"It's not a lie," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. "It's a prescription. I saw myself, Hana. I saw the look on my face when I took your memories. I was... satisfied." 

Hana stopped and looked at him. Her eyes were sharp, but not unkind. "You weren't satisfied because you were evil, Ren. You were satisfied because you were a Broker. You were playing the game Tanaka taught you. Empathy was a limitation, and you pruned it." 

She touched the silver-ringed port at the base of his skull. "But that's not who you are now. You're the man who cut out his own tracer. You're the man who's trying to save the city." 

"I'm the man who knows how to open the cage," Ren said, his eyes fixing on the blueprint. "The Kanda Tower isn't just a link. It’s the 'Central-Stabilizer.' If we can upload the Parent-Key directly into the server-core, the Loom's symmetry breaks. The 'Recorded Future' is overwritten by the present." 

"And how do we get to the core?" Hana asked. 

"We use the 'Director’s Access-Pipe,'" Ren said, pointing to a small, unlisted ventilation shaft at the base of the tower. "It used to be my private route for 'Deep-Zone' harvests. It’s hidden beneath the old mag-lev tracks. To the Master-Loom, it doesn't exist. It’s a ghost-protocol." 

Hana smiled—not the professional smile of the soldier, but the lopsided, genuine smile of the girl in the photo. 

"You really did build the best cage in the world, didn't you?" 

"I built it for myself," Ren said, his hand going to the Diamond Shard. "I just didn't know I’d be the one living in it." 

Suddenly, a sharp, cold sensation hit his ear. His comm-link buzzed into life—not the Syndicate channel, but the colony’s own internal frequency. 

"Elder? Elder, come in!" 

The voice was frantic, sounding like a dying radio. It was Kobi. 

"They're hit! The colony is hit!" 

Ren stood up, his hand going to his Spike-Knife. Beside him, Hana grabbed her Siphon, her breath coming in shallow, frantic bursts. 

"Who hit them?" Ren asked. 

"Easers!" Kobi’s voice roared through the static. "A dozen teams! And they’re not 'Purging,' Ren! They’re 'Syncing'! They’re rewriting the donors!" 

Ren looked at the server-pillar in the center of the cavern. It was glowing with a brilliant, clinical white light. 

The people around the Echo-Piles weren't screaming. They were staring at the light, their eyes rolled back in their heads, their spines connected to the flickering blue vines. 

They weren't being erased. They were being "Recalled." 

"No!" Hana cried, her hand slamming the Sync-Lever of the communal reader. 

It didn't work. The reader was locked. 

"Someone sold the coordinates," Hana hissed, her eyes scanned the crowd. 

Her eyes fixed on a man standing near the Elder’s cradle. He was wearing a worn leather jacket, his skin mapped with the silver scars of a thousand low-level extractions. 

It was Kobi. 

He wasn't on the ground. He was standing with the Easers, his Loom-needle glowing with a malevolent red light. 

"Broker 4-0-9," Kobi said, his voice echoing from the server-pillar’s speakers. "Director Tanaka sends his regards. He says the 'UNRAVEL' phase is nearly over. Give us the Shard, and we can forget the Hollowed ever happened." 

Ren didn't say anything. He didn't have to. The Spike-Knife in his sleeve felt suddenly heavy. 

"You sold them, Kobi," Ren said, his voice sounding like a threat. 

"I didn't sell them, Sato," Kobi laughed, a wet, rattling sound. "I 'Transferred' them. They’re safe now. They’re part of the Loom. They’re part of 'Perfect Symmetry.'" 

He licked his yellowed teeth. "Give it to me, Sato. Tanaka says he’ll let you keep the 'Broker' template if you do. You can have your safehouse back. You can have your status back. You can have your *past* back." 

Ren looked at Hana. She was crying—not for herself, but for her people. 

He didn't hand over the Shard. 

Instead, he grabbed Hana and dove through the drainage tunnel that led to the mag-lev tracks. 

The world turned from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

"Welcome to the 'PURSUE' phase, Kobi," Ren said as the tunnel’s emergency hatch slammed shut. "Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of a Pulse-Wave hit the Shibuya ruins, and the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data.
