# Chapter 3: The Surgeon’s Hand

The air in the lab screamed. It wasn't the scream of a human, but the ultrasonic whine of Ripper blades slicing through the static-heavy atmosphere.

Ren didn't look up. He couldn't afford to. 

"Stay still, Arisawa," Ren commanded, his voice as sharp as the needle in his hand.

The doctor’s head was lolling, his neck muscles too weak to support the weight of his own skull. "They're... they're outside the firewall, Ren. The drones... they’re just the vanguard."

Ren ignored him. He found the port—a relic from the early days of Loom-tech, brass-rimmed and corroded with Verdegris. It was a "Direct-Spinal" interface, far more primitive and painful than the silver-ringed consumer ports of Shinjuku. 

He slid the Loom-needle in.

Arisawa’s entire body arched, a silent spasm of agony that made the life-support cradle groan. On the obsidian console, the swirling light didn't turn violet. It turned a blinding, crystalline white.

"What is this?" Ren hissed, staring at the data-flow. "This isn't trauma. This is... base code."

A Ripper drone plummeted through the shattered skylight, its four rotating blades glowing with a low-frequency hum. It wasn't designed to kill; it was designed to shred memory. Its red sensor eye locked onto Arisawa’s spinal port.

Ren didn't reach for his gun. He reached for a heavy analog tape-reel from the nearest shelf and hurled it.

The metal reel caught the drone’s blades, shattering them in a shower of sparks and magnetic filings. The Ripper tumbled across the floor, its motor screaming as it tried to compensate for the imbalance.

"Hurry!" Arisawa gasped. Blood was beginning to seep from his ears—a sign of "Feedback Rupture." The white light in the Shard was too intense for the doctor’s ancient wiring. "The... the Director... he’s watching through the drone’s optics."

Ren saw the Shard nearing capacity. Forty percent. Sixty. The lab smelled of burning insulation and copper.

Another drone hovered at the skylight, its targeting laser painting a red dot on Ren’s forehead.

Ren ducked, the laser narrowly missing as he slammed a heavy manual override lever on the life-support cradle. The cradle tilted, creating a makeshift shield between him and the hole in the ceiling.

"Eighty percent," Ren muttered, his eyes fixed on the obsidian crystal.

Outside, the sound of heavy boots hitting the data-dust echoed through the ruins. Not drones—Easers. The Syndicate’s elite memory-wipers. They didn't use guns; they used "Blank-Fields" that would reset Ren’s brain to an infant’s before he could pull a trigger.

"Ren..." Arisawa’s voice was a whisper now, barely audible over the mechanical chaos. "The Shard... it’s not for you to sell. It’s for you to... remember."

"I don't have a past to remember, Doctor," Ren said, bracing himself as a blast of Syndicate-ordained EMP hit the building. The lights flickered and died, leaving only the blinding white glow of the Shard.

"You do," Arisawa said. He reached out and grabbed Ren’s hand, his grip surprisingly strong. "You were the first, Ren. The first successful Rebirth. Before you were the Broker, you were the Director. You were the man who built the cage."

Ren froze. The needle in his hand felt like a cold bolt of lightning.

"Impossible," Ren said, his voice flat. "I was a street-harvester. I earned my license in the Grey Zones."

"A lie... implanted after the reset," Arisawa said, his eyes finally clearing for one brief, lucent second. "A masterpiece of narrative-weaving. You’re not hunting a monster, Ren. You’re hunting your own shadow."

The Shard pulsed once, a final, violent surge of white light, and then turned solid. It was no longer obsidian; it was a clear, diamond-like prism that vibrated with a frequency so high it made Ren’s teeth ache.

The lab door was kicked open.

A team of Easers, clad in matte-black suits and carrying high-frequency resonance staves, flooded the room. Behind them stood a tall, slender figure in a tailored grey suit that didn't belong in the ruins.

Director Tanaka.

"Sato-san," Tanaka said, his voice smooth and devoid of malice. "You’ve always had a flair for the dramatic. But I’m afraid Dr. Arisawa is a bit... out of date. Give us the Shard, and we can forget this ever happened."

Ren looked at the diamond Shard in his hand. He looked at Arisawa, who had finally gone limp, his life-signs flatlining on the analog monitor.

He looked at Tanaka, and for the first time in his career, Ren Sato didn't see a client or a handler. 

He saw a target.

"You were the first," Ren whispered, the words echoing in the silence of the lab. 

He didn't hand over the Shard. He tucked it into the hidden compartment of his sleeve and reached for the emergency release on the chemical vapor tanks.

"If I was the first," Ren said as the room filled with freezing fog, "I imagine I know how to break the system better than anyone."
