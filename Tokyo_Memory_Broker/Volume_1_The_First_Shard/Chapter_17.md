# Chapter 17: The Grey Zone War

The Grey Zone wasn't just a place. It was a wound. 

Hana ran through the narrow, rain-slicked alleys, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, rhythmic blue. Beside her, Ren was a blur of duster and focused, mechanical energy. The sound of the Pulse-Wave hitting the ruins behind them was a low, agonizing groan that vibrated in her teeth. 

"They're not just 'Sweeping', Sato," she screamed over the thunder-clap of a nearby Easer-Drone. "They're 'Harvesting'!" 

The Syndicate wasn't just hunting them anymore. They were taking the whole district. They were harvesting the collective trauma of the ruins, turning the Grey Zone into one massive "Fresh Shard" to feed the Loom’s new symmetry. 

Hana saw them through the smoke—rows of Easers, their holographic visors glowing with a brilliant, clinical white. They moved with the synchronized precision of a single mind, their resonance staves singing as they extracted the memories of anyone caught in their path. 

"Down!" Ren roared, grabbing her hand and pulling her into a collapsed arcade. 

A Ripper drone screamed overhead, its red laser-tracer painting the walls of the arcade in a rhythmic, predatory light. 

"The Elder..." Hana gasped, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. "Kobi is... Kobi is giving them the access-codes." 

She saw a group of Hollowed running toward them, their eyes rolling back in their heads, their spines glowing a faint, bioluminescent blue. One of them, a woman Hana had known since she was a child, stopped and reached for her port. 

"Silence," the woman whispered, her voice sounding like a thousand other voices combined. "The Loom is the only... silence." 

Before Hana could move, the woman’s skin turned a dark, bruised violet. She wasn't human anymore. She was a "Sync-Slave"—a biological node in the Loom’s new architecture. 

"They're already in," Ren hissed, his hand going to the Spike-Knife in his sleeve. 

"Then we have to break it," Hana said, her voice sounding suddenly sharp. 

She slammed her Siphon into the arcade’s communal reader, the magnetic spike geysering sparks and high-frequency noise. She was trying to create a "Blank-Field"—a localized noise-pocket that would shield them from the Loom’s sync-pulse. 

But it wasn't enough. 

The Easer team reached the arcade entrance. Their lead enforcer, his matte-black suit dripping with high-grade synthetic lacquer, raised his resonance stave. 

"Yield, Broker 4-0-9," the voice smoothed through the enforcer’s helmet. "The 'ARCHITECT' template is ready. Give us the Shard, and we can finish the promotion." 

Ren didn't yield. 

He didn't use the Spike-Knife. 

He stood up, his hand going to the silver-ringed port at the base of his own skull. 

"Identity," Ren whispered, his voice sounding suddenly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

A high-frequency hum began to resonate from his body. It wasn't the sound of a human heartbeat. It was the "Broker-Pulse"—a specialized signal that only the highest-ranking Syndicate executives knew how to initiate. 

The enforcer’s stave flickered and died. The Easer team stopped, their holographic visors turning a cold, dead grey. 

Ren moved with a fluid, mechanical grace that Hana had never seen before. He walked through the frozen squad, his hands outstretched, his eyes fixing on the Ripper drone above. 

"Feedback Loop," Ren said, his voice a dry rasp. 

Suddenly, the Ripper drone screamed and exploded in a shower of sparks and magnetic filings. The Easer team slumped forward, their neural ports overloaded by a surge of unedited data. 

Ren stopped. His skin was pale, his veins glowing a faint, bioluminescent blue. 

"Sato?" Hana whispered, her hand going to her weapon. 

Ren turned. His eyes were clear, the diamond-like brilliance gone. He looked exhausted. He looked like a man who had just used a muscle he didn't know he had. 

"What was that?" Hana asked, her voice sounding suddenly sharp. 

"The 'Director’s Shield,'" Ren whispered, his hand going to his head. "I didn't... I didn't think I still had the access-codes." 

"You didn't 'have' them, Ren," Hana said, her voice trembling. "You *remembered* them." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the Kanda Tower’s main resonance-pulse hit the district. The sky turned from blue to a blinding, crystalline white. 

"The tower is active," Ren said, his hand going to the Diamond Shard. "Tanaka isn't just harvesting Shibuya. He’s 'Drafting' the city." 

"Then let's go finish it," Hana said, her hand going to his shoulder. 

They began to walk toward the tower, the rain tasting of ozone and old, unedited data. 

As they cleared the arcade, Hana saw a single obsidian shard lying in the data-dust. It was clear, without a single flicker of blue or violet. 

A "Fresh Memory." 

She picked it up. 

"Arisawa’s daughter," the shard whispered, its voice sounding exactly like the Director’s cold-blooded smile. 

"Welcome to the 'RECORDED FUTURE' phase. Try not to get sold." 

Hana crushed the shard in her hand and didn't look back.
