# Chapter 6: The Taste of Truth

The blood on the bathroom floor was a dark, synthetic burgundy. Ren had managed to stitch the wound shut using a makeshift med-kit, but the base of his skull still pulsed with a rhythmic, agonizing heat. 

The Ghost-Thread was gone, crushed beneath the Spike-Knife’s magnetic field. For the first time in ten years, Ren was truly invisible to the Syndicate. 

But invisibility had a price. He was alone, bleeding, and questioning the very nature of his own soul.

He sat on the edge of the tub, the Null-Case in his lap. He reached for the diamond Shard.

It didn't vibrate anymore. It sat in his hand, a cold, crystalline question mark.

As a Broker, Ren knew the risks of "Tasting." It was the process of sampling a Shard’s data directly through his own port. It was the only way to verify purity without a reader, and it was the fastest way to invite "Echo-Rot"—ghost-memories that would eventually overwrite the consumer's base personality.

Brokers who Tasted too often became "Static-Heads," men and women who didn't know if their favorite color was theirs or if it belonged to a donor who died a decade ago.

Ren didn't have a choice. He needed to know what Dr. Arisawa had given his life for.

He closed his eyes and leaned forward. He pressed the flat edge of the diamond against his spinal port.

At first, there was only the familiar hum of the interface. Then, the diamond surged. 

It wasn't a trickle of data. It was a deluge.

Ren’s world didn't fade; it shattered.

He wasn't in the bathroom. He wasn't in Shinjuku.

He was in an operating room—not the crude, analog lab of Arisawa, but a high-end Syndicate facility. The air was sterile, smelling of ozone and high-grade coolant. The lights were a brilliant, clinical white.

"Director Sato," a voice said, amplified by the facility’s comms. "The Loom is stabilized. We are ready to begin the reset."

Ren tried to turn his head. He couldn't. His body was a collection of heavy, unresponsive weights. 

He saw a figure leaning over him. It was Dr. Arisawa, but a decade younger, his face free of the white-noise cataracts. He looked... terrified.

"Is it true, Daisuke?" Arisawa whispered, leaning close to Ren’s ear. "Are you really doing this? Deleting everything you built?"

A second figure stepped into Ren's field of vision. 

It was Director Tanaka. 

He looked exactly the same as he did in the Akihabara deadzone—calm, impeccably tailored, and devoid of any human warmth.

"Trauma is not just a commodity, Arisawa," Tanaka said, his voice smooth and professional. "It is a limitation. If we want the Loom to reach its full potential, we need a vessel that has no memory of the architecture's flaws. We need a Broker who doesn't remember the cost."

Ren felt a sharp, cold sensation at the base of his skull. The Loom-needle. 

"The reset is not a punishment, Ren," Tanaka said, leaning down so his face was inches from Ren's. "It is a promotion. You will be the best in the city because you will be the only one with nothing to lose."

Then, the white light came. 

It wasn't the light of a memory. It was the light of a deletion. 

Ren saw his life unravel. 

He saw himself in a Syndicate boardroom, ordering the mass-extraction of a dissident village. He saw himself in the Akihabara "Grave-Zones," harvesting the first "Self-Death" experiments.

He saw the faces of a thousand donors, their grief and their terror feeding the Loom.

And then, he saw the final image. 

It was a woman. 

She was standing in a rain-slicked Shibuya alley, her face a map of grief and desperation. She was holding a photograph. 

It was the same photograph Hana had. 

"Ren," she whispered. 

The name didn't sound like a name. It sounded like a prayer.

And then, the memory didn't just end. It died. 

Ren saw himself on the surgical table. He saw the monitor flatline. 

He saw Dr. Arisawa pulling the needle out, his hands shaking. 

"He's dead," Arisawa whispered. "Tanaka, you've killed him."

"No," Tanaka said, his voice coming from somewhere above. "We've just cleared the slate. Bring in the 'Broker' template. We have work to do."

Ren’s eyes snapped open. 

He was back in the bathroom. The med-kit was on the floor. The med-bottle was tipped over. 

The diamond Shard was still in his hand, but it was no longer clear. It was a dark, brooding violet.

Ren gasped for air, his lungs feeling like they were filled with static. 

The memory wasn't a fragment. It was a recorded death. 

His death. 

He hadn't been reset. He’d been *replaced*. 

"You were the first," Ren whispered, the words sounding like a death rattle in the silence. 

He wasn't the Director. He wasn't the Broker. 

He was the ghost. 

He looked at the Shard in his hand. The red pulse was gone. 

Instead, a new light was beginning to glow—a soft, pulsing blue that felt like the heartbeat of a person who didn't exist.

Ren Sato stood up. He didn't have a past. He didn't have a future. 

But he had a name. 

And he was going to find the girl in the photograph.
