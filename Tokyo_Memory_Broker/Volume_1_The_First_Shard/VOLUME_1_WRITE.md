# VOLUME_1_WRITE: The First Shard
## Project: Tokyo Memory Broker
## Phase: Volume 1 Design

**Title:** The First Shard  
**Volume Function:** ESTABLISH — Introduce the world of Kiroku, Ren's cynicism, and the "Self-Death" mystery.

---

## 1. VOLUME OVERVIEW

**Dramatic Question:** Is the memory of my death a lie, or is my life an error?  
**Opening State:** Ren is at the top of his game, emotionally numb, harvesting high-value trauma in Shinjuku.  
**Closing State:** Ren is a fugitive from the Syndicate, questioning every memory he owns.

---

## 2. ACT STRUCTURE

### ACT 1: ESTABLISH (Chapters 1–5)
- **Opening Image:** Ren in a high-end "Memory Lounge," watching a client consume a "First Kiss" Shard.
- **The Disruption:** An emergency call to Akihabara. A former Syndicate scientist, Dr. Arisawa, is dying and has a "pure" trauma Shard that needs harvesting.
- **Point of No Return:** Ren extracts the Shard, but before Arisawa dies, he whispers: "You were the first, Ren. Don't let them delete you again."

### ACT 2A: PURSUE (Chapters 6–12)
- **Goal:** Ren tries to verify the Shard's authenticity. He "tastes" a fragment—a professional risk—and sees himself on a surgical table, flatlining.
- **Midpoint Revelation:** He meets Hana, a girl who recognizes his face from a photograph she shouldn't have—a photo of a "Director Sato" from ten years ago.

### ACT 2B: UNRAVEL (Chapters 13–20)
- **The Loss:** The Syndicate realizes the Shard was harvested. They send "Easers" (memory-wipers) after Ren.
- **The Dark Point:** Ren's safehouse is burned. He loses his stash of "Personal Anchor" Shards — the only things he thought were his real history.

### ACT 3: RESOLVE (Chapters 21–30)
- **Decision:** Ren chooses to believe the Shard over his Syndicate handler's lies.
- **Final Confrontation:** Ren uses a "Shared-Trauma" technique to overwhelm the lead Easer, Enforcer Kenji.
- **Ending Revelation:** Ren discovers he has no heartbeat. His body is a high-grade synth-bio vessel. He is a "Live-Data" experiment.

## 4. WRITING SPECIFICATIONS

- **Tone:** Sharp, sensory, atmospheric. Use the rain and neon of Tokyo to reflect the character's internal state.
- **Rules:** Never mention the Year (2084) directly — show it through the tech (Spinal ports, Holo-menus).
- **Voice:** Ren's internal monologue is analytical, often comparing human emotions to market percentages.
