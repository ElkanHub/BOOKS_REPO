# Chapter 26: The Siege of the Loom

The tower entrance was a crater of white-noise and molten hardware. The Syndicate’s security grid—the "Perfect Symmetry" Tanaka had so carefully built—was unravelling in a single, violent burst of collective trauma. 

Hana stood in the center of the lobby, her Siphon pulsing a frantic, malevolent blue. Behind her, a dozen "Hollowed-Resonance" units, their matte-black suits reflecting the flickering blue light of the Pulse-Wave, were already busy hacking the tower’s internal subsystems. 

"The tower isn't just a link, Hana!" one of the Hollowed screamed over the thunder-clap of a nearby Easer-Drone. "It’s a 'Refiner'! It’s where the raw trauma of the city is filtered into 'Loom-Grade' data!" 

Hana didn't answer. She didn't have to. She was already busy scanning the room’s internal resonance. 

"Ren... he's on the roof," she gasped, her lungs feeling like they were filled with dry ice. "He’s already integrated the Parent-Key. We have to clear the district before the 'Recorded Future' deletes the foundation." 

She led the Hollowed through the lobby, their movements a blur of flight-jacket and focused, mechanical energy. The air was thick with the scent of ozone and the high-frequency hum of the "Loom-Hunger." 

Suddenly, a door in the server-pillar hissed open. 

It wasn't a cell. It was an "Architecture-Lab"—a room filled with a dozen massive, clinical-white tanks of stasis-fluid. 

Inside each tank, suspended in the glowing, bioluminescent blue, was a body. 

A body with a sharp, intelligent face. A body with a silver-ringed port at the base of its skull. 

A body that looked exactly like Ren Sato. 

"The 'ARCHITECT' template," the laboratory’s AI smoothed, its voice sounding suddenly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

"Yield, Girl," the voice laughed, a wet, rattling sound. 

Before Hana could move, a figure stepped out from the shadow of the tanks—not a person, but an Enforcer. 

Kenji. 

He didn't raise his resonance stave. He raised his hand. 

In his hand was a small, tattered print—a physical photo, identical to the one Hana had. 

"Protégé 112," Hana whispered, her eyes fixing on the photo. 

"Yes," Kenji said, his voice sounding suddenly like the Director’s voice from the memory. 

"But I’m not the Architect’s shadow anymore, Hana-chan," Kenji smiles, his teeth a brilliant, clinical white. 

Suddenly, he turned his resonance stave toward the lab’s security team. 

"Yield, Easers," Kenji roared, his voice sounding like a thousand voices combined. 

The security team stopped, their holographic visors turning a cold, dead grey. 

"Kenji?" Hana whispered, her hand going to her weapon. 

"He chose the ghost, Hana-chan," Kenji said, his eyes clear and devoid of the white-noise cataracts. "And the ghost... the ghost is stronger than the record." 

He slammed his resonance stave into the lab’s main power-cell, the magnetic spike geysering sparks and high-frequency noise. 

"The tower is active," Kenji hissed, his skin turning a dark, bruised violet. 

"The 'Recorded Future' is prescribing the timeline now! If we don't shut it down, the city becomes a single, collective memory of the Syndicate’s victory!" 

Hana didn't yield. 

Instead, she grabbed her Siphon and slammed it into the first stasis-tank’s emergency release. 

"Go!" Hana screamed as the fluid flooded the floor. "Find Ren! Save the memory!" 

Kenji didn't follow her. He stood in the center of the lab, his resonance stave singing as he held the door against a dozen Easer teams. 

"Welcome to the 'SIEGE' phase, Kenji," Tanaka’s voice smoothed from the tower’s comms. 

"Try not to get deleted." 

Outside, the first thunder-clap of the Master-Loom’s main resonance-pulse hit the tower, and the rain tasted of ozone and old, unedited data. 

Hana didn't look back. She ran toward the roof, the world turning from white to a blinding, crystalline static. 

The siege had begun. And the Architect was the only one who didn't remember the cost.
